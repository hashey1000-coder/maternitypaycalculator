<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Pay for Part-Time Workers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work part-time, you have exactly the same right to maternity leave and pay as full-time employees. There's no minimum number of hours you need to work to qualify for maternity leave — the only earnings test is for Statutory Maternity Pay, which requires average weekly earnings of at least <strong>£129</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Do Part-Time Workers Qualify for SMP?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The key question is whether your <strong>average weekly earnings</strong> meet the Lower Earnings Limit of £129 per week. This is based on your actual pay, not your hourly rate multiplied by contracted hours.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Examples</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>20 hours/week at £12.71/hour</strong>: £254.20/week — <strong>qualifies</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>16 hours/week at £12.71/hour</strong>: £203.36/week — <strong>qualifies</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>12 hours/week at £12.71/hour</strong>: £152.52/week — <strong>qualifies</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>10 hours/week at £12.71/hour</strong>: £127.10/week — <strong>does not qualify</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work variable hours, your average is calculated over the 8-week reference period. If you had a good run of hours in that period, you may still qualify even if some weeks are lower.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your earnings are below £129/week, check your eligibility for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> — the threshold is only £30/week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How SMP Is Calculated for Part-Timers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">SMP is calculated the same way regardless of hours. Your Average Weekly Earnings (AWE) determine your pay:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-6</strong>: 90% of your AWE</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 7-39</strong>: £194.32/week, or 90% of AWE if less</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For part-time workers earning less than approximately £216/week, the 90% rate applies for the entire 39 weeks because 90% of their earnings is less than the £194.32 flat rate.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example: 20 hours at £12.71/hour</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>AWE: £254.20</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks 1-6: 90% × £254.20 = £228.78/week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks 7-39: £194.32/week (since 90% of AWE = £228.78, which exceeds the flat rate)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Total SMP: (6 × £228.78) + (33 × £194.32) = <strong>£7,785.24</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example: 15 hours at £12.71/hour</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>AWE: £190.65</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks 1-39: 90% × £190.65 = <strong>£171.59/week</strong> for all 39 weeks (below flat rate)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Total SMP: 39 × £171.59 = <strong>£6,691.82</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to work out your exact figure.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Equal Treatment Rights</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">As a part-time worker, you have the <strong>same rights</strong> as full-time colleagues:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>52 weeks of maternity leave</strong> — not pro-rated</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP for 39 weeks</strong> — based on your actual earnings</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Protection from dismissal</strong> — during pregnancy and maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Paid antenatal appointments</strong> — at your normal rate</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Annual leave accrual</strong> — pro-rated to your hours, but continues during maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Right to return to the same job</strong> — on the same terms</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Under the Part-Time Workers (Prevention of Less Favourable Treatment) Regulations 2000, your employer cannot treat you less favourably than a comparable full-time worker because of your part-time status.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Enhanced Maternity Pay for Part-Timers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer offers enhanced maternity pay (above SMP), this should also apply to part-time workers on a pro-rata basis. For example, if full-time staff receive 12 weeks at full pay, you should receive 12 weeks at your full part-time pay. The same principle applies to public sector employers — <a href="/guides/nhs-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">NHS staff</a> and <a href="/guides/teacher-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">teachers</a> on part-time contracts receive the enhanced scheme based on their part-time salary.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer's enhanced scheme only applies to full-time workers, or if part-time staff receive a less generous package, this could constitute indirect discrimination under the Part-Time Workers Regulations. Raise any concerns with your employer, your trade union, or contact ACAS on 0300 123 1100 for advice. You have the right to be treated no less favourably than a comparable full-time colleague.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Zero-Hours Contract Workers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're on a zero-hours contract, your maternity pay depends on your average weekly earnings over the 8-week reference period. This can make qualifying for SMP unpredictable, as your hours and pay may fluctuate from week to week. If your average weekly earnings are at least £129, you qualify for SMP — even on a zero-hours contract.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Tips for zero-hours workers approaching maternity:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Try to maintain regular hours during the 8-week reference period (roughly weeks 17-25 of pregnancy) to keep your AWE above £129</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If your AWE falls below £129, you may still qualify for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>, which has a much lower threshold of just £30/week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Keep detailed records of all hours worked and pay received</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Read our dedicated guide on <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-agency-zero-hours-workers/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity pay for agency and zero-hours workers</a> for a full breakdown of your rights and options</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Multiple Jobs and Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work part-time for more than one employer, you may qualify for SMP from each employer if you meet the eligibility criteria with each one. Your earnings from different jobs are <strong>not combined</strong> — each employer assesses your eligibility separately.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't qualify for SMP from any individual employer, you can combine your earnings across jobs when applying for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Returning Part-Time After Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many full-time workers consider switching to part-time hours after maternity leave. You have the statutory right to request flexible working, and your employer must consider your request. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/returning-to-work-after-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">returning to work after maternity leave</a> for detailed advice.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Support for Part-Time Workers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Part-time workers may be particularly affected by the income drop during maternity leave.
