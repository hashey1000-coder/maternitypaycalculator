<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Right to Request Flexible Working</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Since April 2024, <strong>all employees in the UK have the right to request flexible working from day one of their employment</strong>. You no longer need 26 weeks' service. This is particularly relevant for parents returning from maternity leave who need a different working pattern to balance childcare.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can make <strong>two flexible working requests</strong> in any 12-month period, and your employer must respond within <strong>two months</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Types of Flexible Working</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Flexible working isn't just about working from home. You can request:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Part-time hours</strong> — reducing your working days or hours</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Compressed hours</strong> — working full-time hours over fewer days (e.g. 4-day week)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Flexitime</strong> — choosing when you start and finish (within agreed core hours)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Remote/hybrid working</strong> — working from home part or all of the week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Job sharing</strong> — splitting a full-time role between two people</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Staggered hours</strong> — different start, finish, and break times</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Annualised hours</strong> — working a set number of hours per year with flexibility on when</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Term-time working</strong> — working only during school terms</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Make a Flexible Working Request</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your request must be made in writing and include:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>The date</strong> of your request</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>The change</strong> you're requesting (be specific about days, hours, location)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>When you want it to start</strong> (give reasonable notice)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>How it might affect your employer</strong> and how this could be managed</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>A statement</strong> that this is a statutory flexible working request</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">6.</span><span><strong>Whether you've made a previous request</strong> and when</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Sample Flexible Working Request</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">> Dear [Manager's name],
>
> I am writing to make a statutory flexible working request under the Employment Rights Act 1996.
>
> I would like to request a change from my current full-time hours (Monday to Friday, 9am-5pm) to working 3 days per week (Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday, 9am-5pm), starting from [date — at least 8 weeks from your request].
>
> I believe this can work effectively because [explain — e.g. key meetings are Mon-Wed, tasks can be reorganised, a colleague could cover Thurs/Fri queries].
>
> I have not made a previous flexible working request in the last 12 months.
>
> I look forward to discussing this with you.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Your Employer Must Do</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Under the Employment Rights Act 1996 (as amended), your employer must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Deal with your request reasonably</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Consult with you</strong> before making a decision</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Respond within 2 months</strong> (unless you agree to a longer period)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Only refuse for one of 8 statutory business reasons</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">The 8 Reasons an Employer Can Refuse</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>Extra costs that would damage the business</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>Work cannot be reorganised among other staff</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Cannot recruit additional staff</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>Negative impact on quality</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span>Negative impact on performance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">6.</span><span>Detrimental effect on ability to meet customer demand</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">7.</span><span>Insufficient work during the proposed hours</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">8.</span><span>Planned structural changes</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer <strong>cannot</strong> refuse simply because they prefer the status quo or because "it's never been done before."</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tips for a Successful Flexible Working Request</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Plan ahead</strong> — think about it during maternity leave, not just before your return date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Research</strong> — has anyone else in your team or organisation worked flexibly?</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Be specific</strong> — vague requests are harder to approve</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Propose a trial period</strong> — suggest 3 months to demonstrate it works</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Address concerns proactively</strong> — show you've thought about handovers, coverage, etc.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Know your worth</strong> — retention of experienced staff saves employers recruitment costs</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Be prepared to compromise</strong> — you might not get exactly what you ask for</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If Your Request Is Refused?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer refuses your request, they must explain which of the 8 statutory reasons applies and why. You can:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Appeal</strong> through your company's internal process</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Make a new request</strong> (you're entitled to two per year)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Contact ACAS</strong> for free advice (0300 123 1100)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Take a claim to an Employment Tribunal</strong> if the process wasn't followed correctly</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Flexible Working and Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you reduce your hours, your pay will typically be <strong>pro-rated</strong>.
