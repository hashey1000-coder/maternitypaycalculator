<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is Pregnancy Discrimination?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Pregnancy discrimination is when you're treated unfairly because you're pregnant, on maternity leave, or because you've recently given birth. Under the <strong>Equality Act 2010</strong>, pregnancy and maternity are a <strong>protected characteristic</strong>, making discrimination illegal.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Pregnancy discrimination is unfortunately common. Research by the Equality and Human Rights Commission found that <strong>77% of working mothers</strong> reported negative or discriminatory treatment during pregnancy, maternity leave, or on return to work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Types of Pregnancy Discrimination</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Direct Discrimination</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is when you're treated less favourably specifically because of your pregnancy or maternity. Examples include:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being <strong>dismissed</strong> or made redundant because of pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Not being <strong>promoted</strong> because you're pregnant or about to go on maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being <strong>pressured to resign</strong> when you announce your pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Having your <strong>job offer withdrawn</strong> when the employer discovers you're pregnant</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being <strong>excluded from meetings</strong> or training opportunities</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Receiving <strong>negative performance reviews</strong> related to pregnancy absence</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Indirect Discrimination</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is when a workplace policy applies to everyone but disproportionately disadvantages pregnant women or new mothers. Examples include:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A requirement to work <strong>late shifts</strong> that makes it impossible for breastfeeding mothers</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attendance policies that <strong>count pregnancy-related absence</strong> against you</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Performance targets that <strong>don't account for maternity leave</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Harassment</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Pregnancy-related harassment includes:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Colleagues making <strong>unwanted comments</strong> about your pregnancy or body</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being called <strong>unreliable</strong> because of pregnancy appointments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Jokes about you <strong>not coming back</strong> after maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being <strong>ignored or excluded</strong> socially because of your pregnancy</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Victimisation</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is when you're treated badly because you complained about pregnancy discrimination or supported someone else's complaint.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Legal Rights</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Under UK law, you're protected from the moment you become pregnant until the end of your maternity leave (and beyond, in many cases):</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>You cannot be dismissed</strong> for being pregnant or for any reason connected to your pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>You cannot be made redundant</strong> in preference to other employees because of pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If genuine redundancy occurs, you have <strong>priority right to suitable alternative employment</strong> during maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pregnancy-related sickness</strong> cannot be counted in sickness absence records</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You have the right to <strong>all contractual benefits</strong> during maternity leave (except salary)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You have the right to <strong>return to the same job</strong> after ordinary maternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What to Do If You Experience Discrimination</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 1: Keep Detailed Records</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Document everything:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Dates, times, and locations of incidents</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>What was said or done (as close to word-for-word as possible)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Names of witnesses</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Any emails, texts, or written communications</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>How it made you feel and any impact on your health</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 2: Raise It Informally</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you feel comfortable, speak to your line manager or HR department. Sometimes issues can be resolved quickly at this stage, especially if the discrimination is unintentional.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 3: Use the Formal Grievance Process</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If informal resolution doesn't work:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>Submit a <strong>written grievance</strong> following your employer's procedure</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>Attend the <strong>grievance hearing</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Appeal</strong> if you're not satisfied with the outcome</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 4: Contact ACAS</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">ACAS provides free, impartial advice and can help with <strong>Early Conciliation</strong> — a process where ACAS tries to help you and your employer reach agreement without going to tribunal.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>ACAS helpline:</strong> 0300 123 1100</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 5: Employment Tribunal</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If other options fail, you can take your case to an Employment Tribunal. Key points:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must start Early Conciliation within <strong>3 months less 1 day</strong> of the discriminatory act</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Tribunal claims are free to bring</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You can claim <strong>compensation</strong> for financial loss and injury to feelings</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>There is <strong>no cap</strong> on discrimination compensation</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Redundancy During Pregnancy and Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer is making redundancies, special protections apply:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>From the Pregnancy Protection Act 2024, pregnant employees and those on maternity leave have <strong>priority for suitable alternative vacancies</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This priority extends from notification of pregnancy until <strong>18 months after the birth</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must offer you a suitable alternative role before offering it to other at-risk employees</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Being selected for redundancy <strong>because</strong> of pregnancy is automatically unfair dismissal</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read our detailed guide on <a href="/guides/redundancy-during-pregnancy-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">redundancy during pregnancy and maternity</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Getting Legal Support</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you believe you've experienced pregnancy discrimination, several organisations can help you take action:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Maternity Action helpline:</strong> 0808 802 0029 — free, specialist advice on maternity rights and discrimination. They can help you understand your options and draft grievance letters</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>ACAS:</strong> 0300 123 1100 — provides free Early Conciliation to help resolve disputes without going to tribunal</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Citizens Advice:</strong> <a href="https://www.citizensadvice.org.uk" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">citizensadvice.org.uk</a> — can help you understand your legal position and refer you to local solicitors</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Equality Advisory Support Service (EASS):</strong> 0808 800 0082 — specialist equality and discrimination advice</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Working Families helpline:</strong> 0300 012 0312 — legal advice for working parents and carers</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you need to take your case to an Employment Tribunal, you may be able to find a solicitor who works on a <strong>no-win, no-fee</strong> basis for discrimination claims. Legal aid is not usually available for employment cases, but some trade unions provide legal representation as part of membership.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Remember, you must start the ACAS Early Conciliation process within <strong>3 months less 1 day</strong> of the discriminatory act — so don't delay in seeking advice.
