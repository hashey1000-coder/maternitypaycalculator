<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">NHS Maternity Pay and Leave Explained</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work for the NHS, you're entitled to one of the most generous maternity pay packages in the UK — significantly above the <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">statutory minimum</a>. The NHS maternity scheme, governed by the Agenda for Change (AfC) terms and conditions, provides enhanced pay for a substantial portion of your leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">NHS Maternity Pay Rates</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Under Agenda for Change, if you have at least 12 months' continuous NHS service by the beginning of the 11th week before your due date, your maternity pay is:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-8</strong>: Full pay (your normal salary)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 9-26</strong>: Half pay plus SMP (combined, this cannot exceed your full pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 27-39</strong>: SMP only (£194.32/week for 2026/27)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 40-52</strong>: Unpaid</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you have less than 12 months' NHS service, you'll receive SMP only (the same as any other employer).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How Does This Compare to SMP?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For a Band 5 nurse earning approximately £29,970:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Under NHS scheme</strong>: Approximately £19,200 total (8 weeks full + 18 weeks half+SMP + 13 weeks SMP)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP only</strong>: Approximately £9,525 total</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Difference: Over £9,600 extra</strong> from the enhanced scheme</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is why the NHS is considered one of the best employers for maternity pay in the UK.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Eligibility for Enhanced NHS Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To receive the full enhanced maternity pay, you must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Have <strong>12 months' continuous NHS service</strong> by the 11th week before your due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Intend to return to work for <strong>at least 3 months</strong> after your maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Service with different NHS employers counts if there's no break of more than 3 months</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">The Return-to-Work Condition</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The enhanced pay (the half-pay element) is conditional on you returning to NHS employment for at least 3 months. If you don't return, you may have to repay the enhanced portion. The SMP element is not affected — you keep that regardless.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're unsure about returning, discuss your options with your HR department before your leave starts.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">NHS Maternity Leave Entitlements</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">All NHS employees are entitled to the statutory 52 weeks of maternity leave, regardless of length of service. During this time:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You continue to accrue annual leave (including bank holidays)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your incremental date is not affected</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your pension contributions continue during paid leave (employer contributions based on your full pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're protected from redundancy and unfair dismissal</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Pension During NHS Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For NHS Pension Scheme members:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>During paid leave</strong>: Both employer and employee contributions continue. Employee contributions are based on actual pay received, while employer contributions are based on your normal full-time equivalent pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>During unpaid leave</strong>: No contributions are made, but you can choose to buy back the missed pension contributions later</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>KIT days</strong>: Pension contributions apply to KIT day pay</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Annual Leave Accrual</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">NHS staff continue to accrue annual leave during maternity leave. With the NHS offering generous leave allowances (starting at 27 days plus 8 bank holidays, rising to 33 days with long service), this can result in a significant leave balance when you return.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Discuss with your manager how you'll use accrued leave — many NHS staff take it immediately before or after maternity leave for an extended period off.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">KIT Days in the NHS</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Like all employers, the NHS allows up to 10 <a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Keeping In Touch days</a> during maternity leave. Many NHS trusts encourage KIT days for:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Mandatory training updates</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Clinical skills refreshers</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Team meetings and updates</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Appraisals</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your trust should pay you your normal rate for KIT days, on top of your SMP.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Bank and Agency NHS Workers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work for the NHS through a bank or agency, your maternity pay situation is different:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>NHS Bank workers</strong>: You may qualify for the enhanced Agenda for Change scheme if you have 12 months' continuous service with the trust. Some trusts treat regular bank shifts as continuous employment, while others do not. Request written confirmation of your service status from your trust's HR department well before the qualifying week. If you do qualify, you'll receive the same enhanced package as permanent staff</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Agency workers</strong>: You'll generally receive SMP from the agency (not the enhanced NHS rate), provided you meet the standard SMP qualifying conditions — 26 weeks' service and average weekly earnings of at least £129. If you don't qualify for SMP through the agency, apply for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> instead. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-agency-zero-hours-workers/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity pay for agency and zero-hours workers</a> for more detail</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Part-Time NHS Staff</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work part-time for the NHS, your enhanced maternity pay entitlement is exactly the same as full-time colleagues — it is simply based on your contracted part-time salary. Your full pay during weeks 1-8 means your actual part-time salary, and the half-pay element in weeks 9-26 is half of your part-time salary plus SMP. You accrue <a href="/guides/annual-leave-during-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">annual leave</a> on a pro-rata basis throughout your entire maternity leave, including bank holidays. If you're considering changing your hours when you return, you have the right to request <a href="/guides/flexible-working-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">flexible working after maternity leave</a>, and the NHS generally has good policies supporting part-time and flexible arrangements.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Apply</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>Inform your line manager and HR department of your pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>Provide your MATB1 certificate</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Complete your trust's maternity leave application form</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>HR will confirm your pay entitlement and leave dates</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Calculate Your NHS Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">While our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> shows your statutory entitlement, your enhanced NHS pay will be significantly higher. Contact your HR department for an exact breakdown.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Calculate SMP baseline</a> — your statutory minimum</span></li>
