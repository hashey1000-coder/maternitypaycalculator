<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Write a Maternity Leave Letter to Your Employer</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Notifying your employer about your pregnancy and maternity leave plans is a legal requirement, but it can also feel daunting. This guide walks you through exactly what to include, when to send it, and provides free templates you can adapt.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Must You Notify Your Employer?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">By law, you must inform your employer of your pregnancy and intended maternity leave start date no later than the <strong>15th week before your expected week of childbirth</strong> — known as the qualifying week. For most pregnancies, this is around <strong>week 25</strong> of your pregnancy.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, you're not required to tell your employer before this date (though many women choose to). The only exception is if you need to take time off for pregnancy-related appointments or if health and safety adjustments are needed.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to calculate your exact notification deadline.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Your Letter Must Include</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When you formally notify your employer, you must provide three pieces of information:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>That you are pregnant</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Your expected week of childbirth</strong> (your due date)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>When you want your maternity leave to start</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You'll also need to provide your <strong>MATB1 certificate</strong> — the medical certificate your midwife or GP issues from around week 20 of pregnancy. Your employer cannot process your SMP claim without this.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Your Employer Must Do</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Once you've given notice, your employer must respond in writing within <strong>28 days</strong>, confirming:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The date your maternity leave will start</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The date your maternity leave will end (52 weeks from the start)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your SMP entitlement</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If they fail to respond within 28 days, they cannot refuse to let you return to work early.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Leave Notification Letter Template</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Here's a template you can adapt for your situation:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Template 1: Basic Notification</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Dear [Manager's Name],</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I am writing to formally notify you that I am pregnant and wish to take maternity leave. My expected week of childbirth is [date], and I would like my maternity leave to begin on [date].</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I attach my MATB1 certificate as proof of my expected due date.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I understand you will confirm my maternity leave dates and SMP entitlement within 28 days. Please let me know if you need any further information.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Kind regards,
[Your Name]</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Template 2: Detailed Notification with Handover Plan</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Dear [Manager's Name],</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I am writing to let you know that I am pregnant and to formally notify you of my intention to take maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">My expected week of childbirth begins on [date], and my due date is [date]. I would like my maternity leave to start on [date].</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I enclose my MATB1 certificate for your records.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To ensure a smooth transition, I would like to discuss a handover plan over the coming weeks. I am happy to work with you to document my current projects and brief colleagues on ongoing tasks.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">I would be grateful if you could confirm my maternity leave dates and SMP entitlement in writing within the statutory 28-day period.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Kind regards,
[Your Name]</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Changing Your Maternity Leave Start Date</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you change your mind about when you want to start maternity leave, you can change the date as long as you give at least <strong>28 days' notice</strong> of the new date. <li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>You continue working until your chosen start date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Your employer must carry out a workplace risk assessment for pregnant employees</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>You're entitled to paid time off for antenatal appointments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span>You're protected from unfair treatment or dismissal due to pregnancy</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Learn more about your full <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave rights</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Telling Your Employer Informally First</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">While the formal letter is a legal requirement, most women tell their employer informally before sending the written notice — often after their 12-week scan. This gives both sides time to plan.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Tips for the informal conversation:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Choose a private, relaxed setting</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Be clear about your due date and rough plans</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Ask about the company's maternity policy and any enhanced pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Don't feel pressured to commit to a return date immediately</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Follow up the conversation with your formal letter</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights During Pregnancy at Work</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">From the moment you tell your employer you're pregnant, you have legal protections:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Paid time off</strong> for all antenatal appointments (including classes recommended by your midwife)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Health and safety protections</strong> — your employer must assess risks and make reasonable adjustments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Protection from unfair dismissal</strong> — you cannot be dismissed or made redundant because of pregnancy</span></li>
