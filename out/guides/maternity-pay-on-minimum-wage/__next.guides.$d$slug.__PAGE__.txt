<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Pay on Minimum Wage — What You'll Receive</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're earning the National Minimum Wage or National Living Wage, you're probably wondering whether you'll qualify for maternity pay and how much you'll get. The good news is that most minimum wage workers <strong>do</strong> qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay, and there are additional benefits to help top up your income.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">National Living Wage Rates for 2026/27</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">From April 2026, the National Living Wage (for workers aged 21+) is <strong>£12.71 per hour</strong>. If you work full-time (37.5 hours/week), your weekly earnings are approximately <strong>£476.63</strong>, which is well above the Lower Earnings Limit of £129 per week required for SMP.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For part-time workers, the key question is whether your average weekly earnings reach the <strong>£129 threshold</strong>. If you work at least approximately 10.2 hours per week at the National Living Wage, you should qualify.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">SMP on Minimum Wage — The Numbers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you earn the National Living Wage full-time (approximately £24,785/year):</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-6</strong>: 90% of £476.63 = <strong>£428.96 per week</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 7-39</strong>: <strong>£194.32 per week</strong> (the flat rate)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Total gross SMP</strong>: Approximately <strong>£8,986 over 39 weeks</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For your personalised calculation, use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">After Tax</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Because your maternity pay is relatively low, the tax impact is minimal:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week, your annualised earnings are about £10,105 — below the Personal Allowance of £12,570</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This means <strong>zero Income Tax</strong> during the flat-rate period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>National Insurance is also nil during flat-rate weeks (below the Primary Threshold of £242/week)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> for your exact after-tax figures.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Part-Time Workers on Minimum Wage</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work part-time, your SMP depends on your average weekly earnings:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>20 hours/week at £12.71</strong>: £254.20/week → Qualifies for SMP. Weeks 1-6 at £228.78/week, then £194.32/week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>16 hours/week at £12.71</strong>: £203.36/week → Qualifies for SMP. Weeks 1-6 at £183.02/week, then £183.02/week (90% is less than flat rate)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>12 hours/week at £12.71</strong>: £152.52/week → Qualifies for SMP. Weeks 1-6 at £137.27/week, then £137.27/week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>10 hours/week at £12.71</strong>: £127.10/week → <strong>Does not qualify</strong> (below £129 threshold)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't reach the £129 threshold, you should check your eligibility for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> instead.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Additional Benefits for Low-Income Families</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">As a minimum wage earner, you may qualify for several additional benefits that significantly boost your income during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Child Benefit</h3>
<a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a> is available to all parents regardless of income. You'll receive <strong>£27.05/week</strong> for your first child and <strong>£17.90/week</strong> for additional children. The High Income Child Benefit Charge doesn't apply until income reaches £60,000.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Universal Credit</h3>
If your household income drops during maternity leave, you may become eligible for Universal Credit. This can include:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A child element of approximately £3,455/year per child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Help with housing costs</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A childcare element covering up to 85% of childcare costs</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Sure Start Maternity Grant</h3>
If you're on certain benefits and expecting your first child, you can claim a <strong>one-off payment of £500</strong> to help with the costs of a new baby.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Healthy Start Vouchers</h3>
If you're on benefits and pregnant or have children under 4, you can get vouchers worth <strong>£4.25/week</strong> (£8.50 while pregnant) to spend on milk, fruit, vegetables, and infant formula.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Council Tax Reduction</h3>
If your income drops during maternity leave, check with your local council about Council Tax reduction — many councils offer significant discounts for low-income households.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Zero-Hours Contracts and Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work on a zero-hours contract, you may still qualify for SMP if:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You've been continuously employed for 26 weeks by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your average weekly earnings over the 8-week reference period are at least £129</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The challenge with zero-hours contracts is the fluctuation in hours. If your hours have been reduced in the reference period, your AWE may fall below £129. If you don't qualify for SMP, apply for <a href="/guides/maternity-allowance-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Planning on a Low Income</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When money is tight, planning ahead is even more important. Here's what we recommend:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Apply for benefits early</strong> — don't wait until your baby is born to check your entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Claim Child Benefit immediately</strong> after the birth — it can be backdated only 3 months</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Budget for the flat-rate drop</strong> — the transition from your first 6 weeks to the flat rate is significant</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Use KIT days</strong> — you can work up to 10 Keeping In Touch days during maternity leave for extra income</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Check for local support</strong> — many local authorities offer additional grants and support for new families</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read our complete <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">financial planning for maternity guide</a> for more strategies.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights Are the Same</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Regardless of your income level, your <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave rights</a> are identical to higher earners. You're entitled to 52 weeks of leave, protection from dismissal, paid antenatal appointments, and the right to return to your job. Don't let anyone suggest otherwise.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Next Steps</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Calculate your exact SMP</a> with our free calculator</span></li>
