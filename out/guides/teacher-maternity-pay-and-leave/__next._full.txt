<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Pay and Leave for Teachers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're a teacher in England or Wales, your maternity pay is typically more generous than the statutory minimum, thanks to the Burgundy Book conditions of service. However, the exact package depends on whether you work for a local authority school, academy, or free school.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Burgundy Book Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The Burgundy Book, which covers teachers employed by local authority-maintained schools, provides the following maternity pay:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-4</strong>: Full pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 5-6</strong>: 90% of average weekly earnings (the statutory 90% rate)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 7-24</strong>: Half pay plus SMP (combined, this cannot exceed full pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 25-39</strong>: SMP only (£194.32/week for 2026/27)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 40-52</strong>: Unpaid</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for the enhanced pay, you must have at least <strong>one year's continuous service</strong> by the 11th week before your due date and intend to return to work after maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Academy and Free School Teachers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Academies and free schools set their own terms and conditions, which means maternity pay can vary significantly. Some academies mirror the Burgundy Book terms, while others offer different packages. Always check your contract and your academy trust's maternity policy.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Common academy maternity pay arrangements include:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Burgundy Book terms (identical to local authority schools)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Enhanced packages exceeding Burgundy Book terms</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A bespoke trust maternity scheme — some multi-academy trusts have developed their own policies that may offer more or less than the Burgundy Book</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>SMP only (less common but not unheard of, particularly in newer or smaller trusts)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're joining an academy, request a copy of the maternity policy <strong>before</strong> accepting the role, especially if you're planning a family. Your union representative can help you understand how the terms compare with the Burgundy Book. If you're being transferred from a local authority school to an academy through a TUPE transfer, your existing Burgundy Book maternity terms should be protected.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Supply Teachers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Supply teachers face some of the most challenging maternity pay situations in the teaching profession. Your entitlement depends entirely on how you're employed:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employed by a supply agency</strong>: You may qualify for SMP from the agency if you have 26 weeks' continuous service and earn at least £129/week on average. However, gaps between assignments can break continuity, so check your contract carefully</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employed directly by a school on a fixed-term contract</strong>: You may qualify for SMP or even Burgundy Book terms if you have sufficient continuous service</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Self-employed supply teachers</strong>: You won't qualify for SMP, but you can claim <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> if you've been registered as self-employed for at least 26 weeks in the 66-week period before your due date</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're a supply teacher and unsure about your status, read our guide on <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-agency-zero-hours-workers/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity pay for agency and zero-hours workers</a> for more detailed advice on navigating these complex situations.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Teacher Maternity Pay Compares</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For a teacher on a salary of £35,000:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Burgundy Book</strong>: Approximately £19,750 over 39 weeks</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP only</strong>: Approximately £10,050 over 39 weeks</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Difference: Around £9,700 extra</strong> under Burgundy Book terms</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see your SMP baseline.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The Return-to-Work Condition</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Like the NHS, the enhanced half-pay element under Burgundy Book terms is conditional on returning to work for at least <strong>13 weeks</strong> (one term). If you don't return:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must repay the half-pay element</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You keep all SMP received</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You keep the first 4 weeks of full pay</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Some teachers use this strategically — if you're unsure about returning, discuss the financial implications with your union representative before committing.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Planning Your Teaching Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Teachers have unique considerations when planning maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Timing and Terms</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Starting your leave at the beginning of a half-term or term break can maximise your time off</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Consider how your maternity leave interacts with school holidays — you're "on leave" during holidays, but it can feel like bonus time</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If possible, plan so your return coincides with the start of a new term</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Handover</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Prepare detailed schemes of work and resources for your cover</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Brief your cover teacher on individual student needs</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Update your marking and assessment records</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Annual Leave</h3>
Teachers on Burgundy Book terms don't have a separate annual leave entitlement (it's included in school holidays). However, if maternity leave prevents you from taking holiday during term breaks, you may be entitled to additional leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Pension During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Teachers' Pension Scheme members should note:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Employer contributions continue during paid maternity leave based on your actual pensionable pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You can elect to make additional contributions for the unpaid period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>KIT days are pensionable</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">KIT Days for Teachers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Keeping In Touch days</a> can be particularly useful for teachers:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attend INSET days to stay current</span></li>
