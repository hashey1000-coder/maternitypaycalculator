<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Do You Accrue Annual Leave During Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Yes — you <strong>continue to accrue annual leave</strong> throughout your entire maternity leave, including both paid and unpaid weeks. This is one of the most valuable but often overlooked aspects of your <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave rights</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Whether you take 6 months or the full 52 weeks, your holiday entitlement builds up as if you were still at work. For many women, this means returning to work with a substantial bank of unused holiday days.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Holiday Will You Accrue?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The statutory minimum annual leave entitlement in the UK is <strong>5.6 weeks</strong> (28 days for a full-time worker). During 52 weeks of maternity leave, you'll accrue your full year's holiday.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer's holiday year runs January to December and your annual entitlement is 28 days:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You go on maternity leave in March</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You've already taken 5 days of annual leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During your maternity leave (March to March), you accrue 28 days</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You return to work with 23 unused days (28 accrued - 5 already taken)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many employers offer more than the statutory minimum — 25 days plus bank holidays is common. Check your contract or staff handbook.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Using Annual Leave Before Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">A popular strategy is to take annual leave immediately before your maternity leave officially starts. This effectively extends your paid time off because:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Annual leave is paid at your <strong>full salary</strong> (not SMP rates)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>It delays your maternity leave start date, pushing your paid SMP period later</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're well-rested before your maternity leave begins</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example Strategy</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Instead of starting maternity leave at 36 weeks pregnant:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>Use 2 weeks of annual leave from week 36</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>Start maternity leave officially at week 38</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Your SMP now runs 2 weeks later, extending your paid leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to work out your optimal dates.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Using Annual Leave After Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Equally, you can use accrued annual leave at the end of your maternity leave. This means:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You stay off work longer while being paid at full salary</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer is happy because you're using up holiday rather than carrying a large balance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You get a gradual transition — holiday feels different to maternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Carrying Over Annual Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Normally, annual leave must be used within the holiday year. But what if your maternity leave spans two holiday years?</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The law says you must be allowed to carry over annual leave that you couldn't take <strong>because</strong> of maternity leave. Your employer should allow you to carry over any untaken holiday to the following year. This is your legal right — not a discretionary benefit.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, speak to your employer about their policy. Some employers require you to take carried-over leave within a certain period after returning. It's best to agree this in advance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Bank Holidays During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You also continue to accrue bank holiday entitlement during maternity leave. With 8 bank holidays per year in England and Wales, this can add significant extra days. Check whether your employer:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Adds bank holidays to your annual leave balance automatically</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Requires you to take bank holidays as part of your 28-day statutory entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Offers additional days in lieu for bank holidays during maternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tips for Managing Annual Leave and Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Check your holiday year</strong> — know when it starts and ends relative to your maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Calculate your accrual</strong> — work out exactly how many days you'll build up during leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Agree carry-over arrangements</strong> before you go — put it in writing with your employer</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Consider booking leave</strong> before and after maternity leave for maximum time off</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Don't lose days</strong> — ensure your employer records your accrued leave correctly</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The Financial Benefit</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Strategic use of annual leave can significantly boost your maternity leave income. Here's a comparison for someone on a £30,000 salary:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>1 week of annual leave: <strong>£576.92</strong> (full pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>1 week of SMP (weeks 7-39): <strong>£194.32</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Difference: £392.89 per week</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you can use 4 weeks of annual leave (2 before, 2 after maternity leave), that's approximately <strong>£1,571 extra</strong> compared to being on flat-rate SMP for those weeks.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For your exact figures, use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> and <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Employer Cannot Refuse</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer cannot reduce your annual leave entitlement because you're on maternity leave. If they attempt to do so, this would be discrimination. Similarly, they cannot force you to take annual leave during maternity leave — the two are separate entitlements.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you encounter any issues with your annual leave during maternity leave, contact ACAS on 0300 123 1100 for free advice.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Related Resources</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Your maternity leave rights</a></span></li>
