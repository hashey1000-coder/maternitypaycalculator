<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sickness During Pregnancy: What Are Your Rights?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Being ill during pregnancy can be worrying — especially when you're concerned about your job, your pay, and your maternity leave. The good news is that UK employment law provides strong protections for pregnant employees who are off sick.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Pregnancy-Related Illness: Special Protections</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your sickness is related to your pregnancy, you have additional protections beyond the normal sick leave rules:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Your Employer Cannot:</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Include pregnancy-related sickness</strong> in your sickness absence record or use it to trigger disciplinary action</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Dismiss you</strong> for pregnancy-related sickness absence</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Count pregnancy-related absence</strong> towards any attendance management thresholds</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Treat you less favourably</strong> because of pregnancy-related illness</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Your Employer Must:</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Record pregnancy-related absence separately</strong> from other sickness</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Continue your employment</strong> — pregnancy-related sickness absence is protected</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pay you</strong> according to your normal sick pay entitlement</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sick Pay During Pregnancy</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're off sick during pregnancy, you're entitled to Statutory Sick Pay at <strong>£123.25 per week</strong> (2026/27) for up to 28 weeks, provided you earn at least £129 per week. SSP is paid from the 4th day of sickness.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Contractual Sick Pay</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many employers offer sick pay above SSP. If your employer has a contractual sick pay scheme, you're entitled to it during pregnancy-related illness on the same terms as any other sickness. Your employer <strong>cannot</strong> exclude pregnancy-related illness from their sick pay scheme.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Sick Pay and SMP Overlap</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> SSP and SMP cannot be paid at the same time. Once your maternity leave starts (and SMP begins), you stop receiving sick pay and start receiving maternity pay instead.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The 4-Week Trigger: Automatic Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">One of the most important rules to know about pregnancy sickness:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">> If you are off work for a <strong>pregnancy-related illness</strong> in the <strong>4 weeks before your due date</strong>, your maternity leave and SMP start automatically.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is known as the "maternity leave trigger." It means:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must start your maternity leave from the day after your first day of absence</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your SMP starts from the same date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You cannot delay your maternity leave start date</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>What counts as "pregnancy-related"?</strong> This includes any illness caused by or made worse by pregnancy — morning sickness, pelvic girdle pain, high blood pressure, pre-eclampsia, gestational diabetes, etc.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>What doesn't trigger early maternity leave?</strong> Non-pregnancy-related illness (such as a cold, flu, or broken arm) in the 4 weeks before your due date does <strong>not</strong> trigger automatic maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Before the 4-Week Window</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're off sick with a pregnancy-related illness <strong>before</strong> the 4-week window (i.e. more than 4 weeks before your due date), your maternity leave is <strong>not</strong> automatically triggered. Normal sick pay rules apply.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Impact on Your SMP Calculation</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Being off sick can affect your Average Weekly Earnings (AWE), which determines your SMP rate for the first 6 weeks. However:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you receive <strong>full sick pay</strong> during the reference period, your AWE may not be affected</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you receive only <strong>SSP</strong> during the reference period, your AWE will be lower</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The 8-week reference period for AWE is fixed — it depends on when your qualifying week falls</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your illness significantly reduces your AWE, this could lower your SMP during the 90% rate period. Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see the impact based on your actual earnings.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Fit Notes (Sick Notes) During Pregnancy</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're off sick for more than 7 days, you'll need a <strong>fit note</strong> (Statement of Fitness for Work) from your GP. For pregnancy-related illness, your GP should note that the illness is pregnancy-related.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your GP may also suggest:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Phased return</strong> — returning to work with reduced hours</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Amended duties</strong> — changing your role temporarily</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Workplace adaptations</strong> — physical changes to your work environment</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Mental Health During Pregnancy</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Anxiety and depression during pregnancy are common and are treated as pregnancy-related illness. You have the same protections:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Sick absence for pregnancy-related mental health <strong>cannot be counted</strong> against you</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're entitled to the same sick pay as for physical illness</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer should make reasonable adjustments</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read more about your rights in our guide on <a href="/guides/pregnancy-rights-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy rights at work</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If Your Employer Treats You Unfairly?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer counts pregnancy-related absence against you, disciplines you, or treats you less favourably because of pregnancy illness:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This is <strong>pregnancy discrimination</strong> under the Equality Act 2010</span></li>
