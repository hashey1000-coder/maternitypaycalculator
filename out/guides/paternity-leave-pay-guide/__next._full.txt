<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Paternity Leave and Pay — A Guide for Fathers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Becoming a father is an exciting time, and UK law provides specific rights for new dads (and partners) to take time off work. This guide covers everything you need to know about paternity leave and Statutory Paternity Pay for 2026/27.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is Paternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Paternity leave gives eligible employees the right to take either <strong>one or two consecutive weeks</strong> off work when their baby is born (or when an adopted child is placed with them). From April 2024, paternity leave became more flexible — you can now take it in two separate one-week blocks rather than as a single continuous period.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Key Changes from April 2024</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Paternity leave can be taken in <strong>two separate one-week blocks</strong> (previously had to be consecutive)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Leave can be taken at <strong>any point in the first year</strong> after birth (previously had to be within 56 days)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Notice period reduced to <strong>28 days</strong> before each week of leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Qualifies for Paternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for Statutory Paternity Leave, you must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Be an <strong>employee</strong> (not self-employed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Have worked for your employer for at least <strong>26 continuous weeks</strong> by the end of the 15th week before the due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Be the baby's <strong>biological father</strong>, or the mother's <strong>partner</strong> (including same-sex partners)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Be taking leave to care for the baby or support the mother</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Statutory Paternity Pay (SPP) Rates</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For 2026/27, Statutory Paternity Pay is:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£194.32 per week</strong> or <strong>90% of your average weekly earnings</strong> — whichever is lower</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Paid for <strong>one or two weeks</strong> (matching the leave you take)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Subject to Income Tax and National Insurance deductions</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for SPP, you must earn at least <strong>£129 per week</strong> on average.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">After Tax</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">At £194.32/week, SPP is below the NI Primary Threshold (£242/week), so you won't pay National Insurance on it. Income Tax will depend on your cumulative earnings for the tax year, but the amount is usually minimal.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim Paternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Tell your employer</strong> at least 15 weeks before the due date that you want to take paternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Complete form SC3</strong> (or your employer's own form) stating your name, the expected due date, how much leave you want, and when</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Give 28 days' notice</strong> before each block of leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>Your employer <strong>cannot refuse</strong> your request if you meet the eligibility criteria</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Paternity Leave vs Shared Parental Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If two weeks of paternity leave isn't enough, consider <a href="/guides/shared-parental-leave-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Shared Parental Leave (SPL)</a>. SPL allows the mother to convert unused maternity leave and pay into shared leave that either parent can take.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">With SPL, fathers can take up to <strong>50 weeks of leave</strong> (shared with the mother) and receive up to <strong>37 weeks of Shared Parental Leave Pay</strong> at £194.32/week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Which Is Better?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Paternity leave</strong> is simpler and guaranteed — take 1 or 2 weeks without affecting the mother's entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Shared Parental Leave</strong> offers far more flexibility and longer time off, but requires the mother to end her maternity leave early</span></li>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Claim Child Benefit</a></strong> — worth £27.05/week for your first child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Budget for the gap</strong> — if your normal weekly pay is significantly above £194.32, plan for the reduction</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Consider timing</strong> — with the new flexibility, you can time your leave for when it's most needed</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Paternity Leave Rights</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">During paternity leave, you have the same employment protections as during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your job is protected — you're entitled to return to the same role</span></li>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're protected from unfair dismissal related to taking paternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your pension contributions continue (employer contributions based on normal pay)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Self-Employed Fathers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Unfortunately, self-employed fathers have no statutory right to paternity leave or pay.
