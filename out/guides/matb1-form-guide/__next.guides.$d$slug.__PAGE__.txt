<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is a MATB1 Form?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The MATB1 (Maternity Certificate) is an official medical document issued by your midwife or doctor that confirms your pregnancy and expected due date. It's essential for claiming Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) from your employer or Maternity Allowance from the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer <strong>cannot</strong> process your SMP without a MATB1 certificate. If you're claiming Maternity Allowance instead, you'll need to include your MATB1 with your MA1 application form.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Do You Get Your MATB1 Certificate?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your midwife or GP can issue your MATB1 from <strong>20 weeks of pregnancy</strong> onwards. Most women receive theirs at their <strong>20-week anomaly scan</strong> or shortly after.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You should aim to get your MATB1 well before the <strong>15th week before your due date</strong> (around week 25 of pregnancy), as this is the deadline for notifying your employer about your maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Key Dates to Remember</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>20 weeks</strong> — your MATB1 can be issued from this point</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>20-25 weeks</strong> — collect your MATB1 from your midwife or GP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>25 weeks</strong> — deadline to notify your employer (the 15th week before your expected week of childbirth)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>29 weeks</strong> — the earliest your maternity leave can start (11 weeks before your EWC)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to calculate all of these key dates automatically based on your due date.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Information Is on the MATB1?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The MATB1 certificate contains:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your name</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your expected week of childbirth (EWC) — this is the week your baby is due, starting on a Sunday</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The date the certificate was signed</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Whether the baby has already been born (if applicable)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The signature and professional details of the midwife or doctor</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> The MATB1 states your "expected week of childbirth" which starts on the <strong>Sunday</strong> before your due date. This is a specific legal term used for SMP eligibility calculations.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Get Your MATB1</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Ask your midwife</strong> at your 20-week appointment or any routine antenatal visit after that</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Ask your GP</strong> if you haven't been able to get one from your midwife</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Hospital consultants</strong> can also issue MATB1 certificates</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The MATB1 is free. You should never be charged for it. If your original is lost, your midwife or doctor can issue a replacement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Does Your Employer Do with the MATB1?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When you submit your MATB1 to your employer, they use it to:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Verify your due date</strong> for calculating the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Confirm SMP eligibility</strong> — you must have been employed for 26 weeks by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Set up SMP payments</strong> through payroll</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Claim SMP reimbursement</strong> from HMRC (employers can reclaim 92-103% of SMP)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer must keep the MATB1 for at least 3 years after the end of the tax year in which SMP was paid.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Happens After You Submit Your MATB1?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Once your employer receives your MATB1, the process of confirming your maternity leave and pay begins. Your employer must respond to your maternity leave notification within <strong>28 days</strong>, confirming your expected return date. They will pass your MATB1 to their payroll department to set up your <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay</a> payments.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If there are any issues with your MATB1 — for example, if the dates don't match your notification letter — your employer should contact you to resolve it. If your employer believes you don't qualify for SMP, they must provide you with an <strong>SMP1 form</strong> explaining why, so you can claim <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> instead.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">It's a good idea to submit your MATB1 at the same time as your <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-letter-to-employer/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave letter</a>. This keeps things simple and ensures your employer has everything they need in one go. Keep a photocopy or photograph of the original MATB1 for your records before handing it over.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Do You Need the Original MATB1?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Ideally yes — your employer may request the original certificate. However, a copy may be accepted by some employers. For Maternity Allowance claims to the DWP, you must send the original MATB1 (it will be returned to you).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work for multiple employers or need the form for different purposes, ask your midwife for additional copies.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">MATB1 and Maternity Allowance</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're claiming <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> instead of SMP, you need to include your MATB1 with your <strong>MA1 application form</strong>. Send the original — the DWP will return it.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can apply for Maternity Allowance from the 26th week of pregnancy. Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance Calculator</a> to estimate your weekly payment.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If You Can't Get a MATB1?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">In rare cases where you can't obtain a MATB1 (for example, if you're receiving maternity care abroad), your employer or the DWP may accept alternative medical evidence. Contact HMRC (for SMP queries) or the DWP (for MA) to discuss your situation.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Key Things to Remember</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Get your MATB1 from <strong>20 weeks</strong> of pregnancy</span></li>
