<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Rights for Agency Workers and Zero-Hours Contracts</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work through an employment agency or on a zero-hours contract, you <strong>do</strong> have maternity rights — though the specifics depend on your employment status and earnings. Many women in these roles don't realise they may qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay or Maternity Allowance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Agency Workers: SMP Eligibility</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Agency workers can qualify for SMP if they meet the same criteria as any other employee:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>26 weeks' continuous service</strong> with the same agency by the qualifying week (15th week before due date)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Earnings above the Lower Earnings Limit</strong> of £129 per week on average</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The agency is your employer for SMP purposes (which it usually is)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Key point:</strong> Continuous service means being employed by the agency, even if you've worked at different placements. A gap between assignments doesn't necessarily break continuity if there's no formal termination of your agency contract.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">What If You Don't Qualify for SMP Through Your Agency?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't meet the SMP criteria, you may still qualify for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>. MA has more flexible eligibility rules:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You need to have worked for <strong>at least 26 of the 66 weeks</strong> before your due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Work for <strong>any</strong> employer counts — it doesn't need to be continuous with one employer</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You need to have earned at least <strong>£30 per week</strong> in at least 13 of those weeks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This makes MA particularly suitable for agency workers who move between assignments.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Zero-Hours Contract Workers: Your Rights</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Zero-hours contract workers are usually classed as <strong>workers</strong> or <strong>employees</strong> depending on their contract terms. Either way, you have maternity rights:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">If You're an Employee on a Zero-Hours Contract</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You qualify for the same SMP rights as any other employee. The key requirements are:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>26 weeks' continuous employment by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Average weekly earnings of at least £129 (calculated over the 8-week reference period)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Earnings calculation:</strong> If your hours vary, HMRC calculates your average weekly earnings using the 8-week pay period ending with the last normal pay day on or before the Saturday at the end of the qualifying week. This means weeks where you worked fewer hours are still included.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">If You're a Worker (Not an Employee)</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Workers who aren't employees can't claim SMP, but they can usually claim Maternity Allowance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Calculate Your Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our free calculators:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> — enter your earnings to see your SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance Calculator</a> — check your MA entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/calculators/smp-vs-maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">SMP vs Maternity Allowance</a> — compare which you qualify for</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If Your Agency Says You Don't Qualify?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your agency refuses SMP and you believe you're eligible:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>They must give you a <strong>form SMP1</strong> explaining why you don't qualify</span></li>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>52 weeks' maternity leave (26 ordinary + 26 additional)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Protection from unfair dismissal due to pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The right to return to the same or similar job</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Workers</strong> may not have the same leave rights, but they cannot be subjected to a detriment or dismissed for pregnancy-related reasons.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Support If You Don't Qualify for SMP or MA</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't qualify for either <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay</a> or <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>, you may still be entitled to financial support:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong> — you can apply if you're on a low income or out of work.
