<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Getting a Mortgage While on Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Applying for a mortgage while on maternity leave used to be extremely difficult, with many lenders refusing to consider maternity pay as income. Fortunately, the rules have changed significantly, and discrimination against women on maternity leave is now unlawful. However, the process still requires careful preparation.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Can Lenders Refuse You Because of Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">No — under the <strong>Equality Act 2010</strong>, mortgage lenders cannot discriminate against you because you're on maternity leave. In 2019, the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) issued guidance making it clear that lenders should assess maternity leave applications based on your <strong>return-to-work income</strong>, not your temporary maternity pay.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If a lender refuses your application solely because you're on maternity leave, this could constitute discrimination.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Lenders Assess Maternity Income</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Most lenders now take one of two approaches:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Approach 1: Return-to-Work Salary</h3>
The majority of mainstream lenders will base their affordability assessment on the salary you'll return to after maternity leave. You'll typically need to provide:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A letter from your employer confirming your return-to-work salary and date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your last three months' payslips before maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your P60 for the previous tax year</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Details of your maternity pay schedule</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Approach 2: Current Income</h3>
A small number of lenders may assess based on your current maternity pay, which will significantly reduce the amount you can borrow. Avoid these lenders if possible.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tips for a Successful Application</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Use your return-to-work salary</strong> — get a letter from your employer confirming your salary and intended return date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Choose the right lender</strong> — work with a mortgage broker who understands maternity leave applications (not all lenders are equal)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Apply with a partner</strong> — if applying jointly, the non-maternity-leave income strengthens the application</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Get your documents ready</strong> — pre-maternity payslips, P60, employer's letter, and bank statements</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Reduce other debts</strong> — pay down credit cards and loans to improve affordability ratios</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">6.</span><span><strong>Consider the timing</strong> — some women find it easier to apply just before starting maternity leave when payslips still show full salary</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Can You Borrow?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If the lender uses your return-to-work salary, your borrowing capacity should be similar to a normal application. Most lenders offer 4-4.5 times your annual income:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£30,000 salary</strong>: Up to £135,000</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£40,000 salary</strong>: Up to £180,000</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£50,000 salary</strong>: Up to £225,000</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Joint application (£30k + £35k)</strong>: Up to £292,500</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to understand your income during leave, and plan your mortgage payments around the temporary income reduction.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Mortgage Payments During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">While you can get a mortgage, you need to plan carefully for the reduced income during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your SMP will be significantly lower than your normal salary — use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to see exactly what you'll receive each month</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Budget for mortgage payments using your maternity pay figures from our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Consider building a savings buffer of at least 3-6 months' mortgage payments before your maternity leave starts</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Check if your mortgage product allows payment holidays or temporary reduced payments — many lenders offer these, though interest continues to accrue</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Practical Budgeting Tips for Mortgage Holders</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Managing a mortgage on maternity pay requires proactive planning. Here are strategies that can help:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Overpay before maternity leave</strong>: If your mortgage allows it, make overpayments while you're on full salary. This reduces your outstanding balance and may give you the option to underpay later</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Switch to interest-only temporarily</strong>: Some lenders allow a temporary switch to interest-only payments, which can halve your monthly outgoing. Speak to your lender well in advance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Review all household outgoings</strong>: Cancel unused subscriptions, switch utility providers, and reduce discretionary spending to free up cash for mortgage payments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Use <a href="/guides/annual-leave-during-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">annual leave</a> strategically</strong>: Taking accrued holiday before or after maternity leave means weeks at full salary instead of SMP, easing the pressure on mortgage payments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Claim all <a href="/guides/benefits-during-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">benefits you're entitled to</a></strong>: Child Benefit, potential Universal Credit top-ups, and other support can help cover the shortfall</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read our <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">financial planning for maternity guide</a> for comprehensive budgeting advice.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Remortgaging on Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're remortgaging rather than buying, the same principles apply — most lenders will use your return-to-work salary. However, your existing payment history works in your favour, as it demonstrates you can afford the repayments.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What to Do If You're Refused</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If a lender refuses your application because of maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Ask for the reason in writing</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Complain to the lender</strong> — their decision may violate equality law</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Contact the Financial Ombudsman Service</strong> — they handle complaints about unfair mortgage decisions</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Try a different lender</strong> — experiences vary significantly</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Use a specialist broker</strong> — they know which lenders are maternity-friendly</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Child Benefit and Mortgage Applications</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a> income can sometimes be included in mortgage affordability assessments. While it's a small amount, every bit helps. Make sure to mention it to your mortgage adviser.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Related Resources</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Calculate your maternity pay</a> — understand your income during leave</span></li>
