<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Benefits You Can Claim During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Taking maternity leave often means a significant drop in household income. Fortunately, the UK benefits system provides several forms of support for new and expectant parents. This guide covers every benefit you may be entitled to during pregnancy and maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Child Benefit</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a> is the most widely claimed family benefit and is available to <strong>all parents regardless of income</strong>. For 2026/27:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>First child</strong>: £27.05 per week (£1,406.60/year)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Each additional child</strong>: £17.90 per week (£930.80/year)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Claim as soon as your baby is born. It can only be backdated 3 months, so don't delay. Even if the higher earner in your household earns over £60,000, you should still register to protect your National Insurance record.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit Calculator</a> to see your exact entitlement including any High Income Charge.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Universal Credit</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Universal Credit is a means-tested benefit that can provide significant support during maternity leave, especially when your income drops to <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">SMP levels</a>. It can help with:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Standard allowance</strong>: Up to £393.45/month (single, over 25) or £617.60/month (couple)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Child element</strong>: Up to £287.92/month per child (first and second child)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Housing costs</strong>: Help with rent payments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Childcare element</strong>: Up to 85% of childcare costs (capped at £1,014.63/month for one child)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Does SMP Count as Income?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Yes — both SMP and Maternity Allowance are counted as income for Universal Credit purposes. However, because your maternity pay is lower than your normal salary, you may still qualify for UC or receive a higher payment than usual.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can use the government's <a href="https://www.gov.uk/benefits-calculators" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">benefits calculator</a> to estimate your entitlement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sure Start Maternity Grant</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The Sure Start Maternity Grant provides a <strong>one-off payment of £500</strong> to help with the costs of a new baby. You may qualify if:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You or your partner receive certain benefits (Universal Credit, Income Support, Pension Credit, etc.)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're expecting your first child (or in some cases, additional children)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You claim within <strong>11 weeks of the due date or 6 months after the birth</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The grant doesn't need to be repaid and can be spent on anything you need for your baby.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Healthy Start Vouchers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're on certain benefits, you can get Healthy Start vouchers to help with the cost of food:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pregnant women</strong>: £4.25 per week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Babies under 1</strong>: £8.50 per week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Children aged 1-4</strong>: £4.25 per week</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Vouchers can be spent on milk, fruit, vegetables, infant formula, and vitamins at participating shops.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tax-Free Childcare</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When you return to work and need childcare, you may be eligible for Tax-Free Childcare:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The government tops up your childcare payments by <strong>25%</strong>, up to £2,000/year per child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Available for children up to age 11 (17 if disabled)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Both parents must normally be working (or on maternity leave)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You cannot claim Tax-Free Childcare and Universal Credit childcare support at the same time — check which is more beneficial</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Free Prescriptions and Dental Care</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">All pregnant women and new mothers are entitled to <strong>free NHS prescriptions and dental care</strong>. This continues for <strong>12 months after your baby is born</strong>. You'll need a maternity exemption certificate — ask your midwife or GP for form FW8.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Council Tax Reduction</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your household income drops during maternity leave, you may qualify for a reduction in your Council Tax. The rules vary by local authority, so contact your council directly to check.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Housing Benefit / Universal Credit Housing Element</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're renting and your income drops during maternity leave, you may qualify for help with your rent through:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit housing element</strong> (if you're claiming UC)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Housing Benefit</strong> (if you haven't moved to UC yet)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Working Tax Credit / Child Tax Credit</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're still receiving Tax Credits (rather than Universal Credit), your payments may increase during maternity leave due to your reduced income. Report your change in circumstances to HMRC.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Free School Meals and Early Years Funding</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Looking ahead, low-income families may qualify for:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>15 hours free childcare</strong> from age 2 (30 hours from age 3 for working parents)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Free school meals</strong> for eligible families</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Employer Benefits During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Don't forget to check what your employer provides:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Enhanced maternity pay</strong> above SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Company benefits</strong> that continue during leave (private health, life insurance)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pension contributions</strong> — your employer must continue contributing during paid leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Annual leave accrual</strong> — you keep building holiday</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Learn more about your rights in our <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave rights guide</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a></strong>: Online or by post using form CH2 after the birth</span></li>
