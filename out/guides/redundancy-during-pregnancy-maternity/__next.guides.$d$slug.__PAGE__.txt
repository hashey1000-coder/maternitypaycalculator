<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Can You Be Made Redundant While Pregnant or on Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Being told about redundancy during pregnancy or maternity leave is every working mother's nightmare. While redundancy <strong>can</strong> happen during this period (it's not completely prohibited), you have <strong>significantly stronger protections</strong> than other employees. Understanding these rights is crucial.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Enhanced Redundancy Protections</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">From April 2024, the UK government strengthened protections for pregnant workers and those on maternity leave. The key protection is <strong>priority for suitable alternative vacancies</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">When Does the Protection Apply?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You have enhanced protection:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>From the moment you <strong>inform your employer you're pregnant</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Throughout your <strong>entire maternity leave</strong> (up to 52 weeks)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>For <strong>18 months after the expected week of childbirth</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">What Does "Priority" Mean?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your role is made redundant and there is a suitable alternative vacancy, your employer <strong>must offer it to you</strong> before considering other employees. This isn't just a preference — it's a legal requirement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">A suitable alternative vacancy is one that:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Is appropriate for you to do</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Has terms and conditions no less favourable than your current role</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Starts immediately after your current role ends</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Redundancy Is Unlawful</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Redundancy is <strong>automatically unfair dismissal</strong> if:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The real reason for the redundancy is your pregnancy or maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You were selected for redundancy because of your pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer failed to offer you a suitable alternative vacancy that exists</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The redundancy selection criteria indirectly discriminate against you (e.g., using attendance records that include pregnancy-related absences)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights During the Redundancy Process</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Consultation</h3>
Your employer must:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Follow a fair consultation process</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Explain why your role is at risk</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Consider alternatives to redundancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Give you adequate time to respond</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Not use pregnancy-related factors in selection criteria</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">During Pregnancy</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must be considered for all suitable alternative roles first</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Pregnancy-related absences must not count against you</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must not pressure you to accept less favourable terms</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">During Maternity Leave</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The same priority for alternative vacancies applies</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You should be kept informed of developments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You can attend consultation meetings (these can count as <a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">KIT days</a>)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Redundancy Pay and Maternity</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you are genuinely made redundant, you're entitled to:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Statutory Redundancy Pay</strong> — based on your length of service, age, and weekly pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Notice pay</strong> — or pay in lieu of notice</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Outstanding SMP</strong> — if you're still within the 39-week paid period, your SMP continues even after redundancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Accrued annual leave</strong> — payment for any unused holiday</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">SMP After Redundancy</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is a crucial point: if you're made redundant during maternity leave, your employer must still pay your remaining SMP. If they can't (for example, if the company is insolvent), HMRC will pay it directly.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What to Do If You're Facing Redundancy</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Don't panic</strong> — your protections are strong</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Get everything in writing</strong> — request written confirmation of the redundancy reason and process</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Check for suitable alternatives</strong> — ask your employer what other roles are available</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Contact ACAS</strong> — call 0300 123 1100 for free advice</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Speak to your union</strong> — if you're a member, they can represent you</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">6.</span><span><strong>Take legal advice</strong> — many employment solicitors offer free initial consultations</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">7.</span><span><strong>Keep records</strong> — save all emails, letters, and notes of conversations</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">8.</span><span><strong>Know the time limits</strong> — you have 3 months less a day from the dismissal date to bring an Employment Tribunal claim</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Pay If You're Made Redundant</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your SMP or <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> entitlement continues even if you're made redundant:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP</strong>: Your employer must continue paying for the full 39-week entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>MA</strong>: Not affected by redundancy as it's paid by the DWP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a></strong>: Completely unaffected</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to check your ongoing entitlement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Constructive Dismissal</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer makes your working conditions so intolerable that you feel forced to resign because of your pregnancy, this may be <strong>constructive dismissal</strong> — which can be treated as automatically unfair dismissal. Examples include:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Removing responsibilities or demoting you</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Excluding you from meetings or decisions</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Creating a hostile work environment</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Refusing to make reasonable adjustments for your pregnancy</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Getting Support</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're facing redundancy while pregnant or on maternity leave, don't try to navigate it alone. Several organisations offer free, confidential support:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>ACAS</strong>: 0300 123 1100 — free, impartial advice on your rights and the redundancy process. They can also provide early conciliation if you need to make a tribunal claim</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Citizens Advice</strong>: citizensadvice.org.uk — free legal guidance including help with understanding your redundancy package and next steps</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Maternity Action</strong>: maternityaction.org.uk — a specialist maternity rights charity with a free advice line (0808 802 0029) staffed by experts in pregnancy and maternity discrimination</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Your trade union</strong>: If you're a member, your union can represent you in consultations, negotiate on your behalf, and support you through a tribunal claim if needed</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employment solicitor</strong>: Many offer a free initial consultation and work on a no-win, no-fee basis for <a href="/guides/pregnancy-discrimination-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy discrimination</a> and unfair dismissal claims</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Act quickly — the time limit for bringing an Employment Tribunal claim is <strong>3 months less one day</strong> from the date of dismissal. Contact ACAS for early conciliation as soon as possible if you believe the redundancy is unfair.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Related Guides</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Your maternity leave rights</a></span></li>
