<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The Financial Impact of Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Taking maternity leave will likely mean a significant reduction in your income. Understanding exactly how much you'll receive is the first step in planning.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see your exact weekly breakdown, then follow this guide to plan your finances.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 1: Calculate Your Income</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Maternity Pay</h3>
Work out exactly what you'll receive:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-6</strong>: 90% of your average weekly earnings</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 7-39</strong>: £194.32 per week (or 90% of AWE if lower)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 40-52</strong>: Unpaid</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Partner's Income</h3>
Factor in your partner's earnings and any shared parental leave they might take.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Benefits</h3>
Check your eligibility for:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a> — up to £27.05/week for first child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Universal Credit</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Tax-Free Childcare</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Sure Start Maternity Grant (£500 for first child if on benefits)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 2: Audit Your Expenses</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Essential Expenses</h3>
List your non-negotiable monthly costs:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Mortgage/rent</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Council tax</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Utilities (gas, electric, water)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Insurance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Food and groceries</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Transport</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Flexible Expenses</h3>
Identify spending you could reduce:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Subscriptions (streaming, gym, magazines)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Eating out and takeaways</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Clothing and shopping</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Entertainment</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">New Baby Costs</h3>
Budget for new expenses:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Nappies: approximately £40-60/month</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Formula (if needed): approximately £40-80/month</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Baby essentials and clothing</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Childcare (when you return to work)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 3: Build a Maternity Leave Budget</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Create a month-by-month budget covering your entire maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Month 1-1.5 (Higher rate SMP)</strong>
Your income will be 90% of normal — closest to your usual salary.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Months 2-9 (Standard rate SMP)</strong>
This is when the financial impact hits hardest. You'll receive £194.32/week (approximately £810/month before tax).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Months 10-12 (Unpaid)</strong>
If you take the full 52 weeks, the last 13 weeks are completely unpaid.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 4: Start Saving Early</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How Much to Save</h3>
Calculate the monthly shortfall between your maternity income and expenses, then multiply by the number of months you plan to take off.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Example:</strong>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Monthly expenses: £2,500</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Monthly maternity pay (standard rate): £797</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Monthly shortfall: £1,703</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If on standard rate for 8 months: £1,703 × 8 = <strong>£13,624 to save</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Saving Strategies</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Set up a standing order</strong> to a dedicated savings account — automate this so you save without thinking about it</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Reduce unnecessary spending</strong> immediately — review your bank statements and cancel anything you don't use regularly</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Sell items you no longer need</strong> — use online marketplaces to generate extra cash from clothes, electronics, and furniture</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Start a no-spend challenge</strong> — try one or two no-spend weeks per month to boost your savings</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Redirect any bonuses or overtime</strong> pay directly into your maternity savings fund</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Consider a cash ISA</strong> — if you're saving well in advance, earn tax-free interest on your maternity fund</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to calculate the exact income gap you need to cover, and our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to see your after-tax maternity pay.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 5: Reduce Costs Before Baby Arrives</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Start cutting costs as early as possible to stretch your savings further:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Overpay your mortgage</strong> to build up a payment holiday buffer — speak to your lender about options. If you're worried about your <a href="/guides/mortgage-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">mortgage during maternity leave</a>, plan ahead.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Switch energy providers</strong> to reduce bills</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Review insurance</strong> policies for better deals</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Cancel unused subscriptions</strong> — streaming services, gym memberships, magazine subscriptions</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Batch cook and freeze</strong> meals for after the birth — this saves money and time when you're sleep-deprived</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Accept hand-me-downs</strong> — babies grow fast and barely wear most clothes</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Buy second-hand</strong> where safe to do so — items like moses baskets, prams, and baby clothes</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read our guide on <a href="/guides/how-much-does-a-baby-cost-uk/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">how much a baby costs in the UK</a> for a detailed breakdown of what to budget for.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 6: Maximise Your Benefits</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Child Benefit</h3>
<a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Calculate your Child Benefit</a> — it is worth up to £27.05 per week for your first child in 2026/27. Even if you or your partner earns over £60,000 and is subject to the High Income Child Benefit Charge, it is still worth claiming to protect your National Insurance record and State Pension entitlement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Tax-Free Childcare</h3>
Open a <a href="/guides/tax-free-childcare-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Tax-Free Childcare</a> account before your return to work. The government tops up every £8 you pay with £2, saving you up to £2,000 per year per child.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Sure Start Maternity Grant</h3>
If you are on certain benefits and expecting your first child, you may be eligible for a one-off <a href="/guides/sure-start-maternity-grant/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Sure Start Maternity Grant</a> of £500.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Annual Leave</h3>
Remember you accrue <a href="/guides/annual-leave-during-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">annual leave during maternity leave</a>. You can use this to extend your paid time off or receive a payout. This can add several extra weeks of income to your maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Step 7: Universal Credit and Benefits During Maternity</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your income drops significantly during maternity leave, you may become eligible for additional financial support:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong> — you may be able to claim if your household income falls low enough. <li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Council Tax Reduction</strong> — your local authority may reduce your council tax bill based on your reduced income during maternity leave</span></li>
