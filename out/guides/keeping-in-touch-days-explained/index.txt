<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Are Keeping In Touch (KIT) Days?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Keeping In Touch days — commonly known as KIT days — allow you to work for up to <strong>10 days</strong> during your maternity leave without it ending your leave or affecting your Statutory Maternity Pay. They're designed to help you stay connected with your workplace and ease the transition back to work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">KIT days are completely <strong>voluntary</strong> — your employer cannot require you to work, and you cannot insist on working. Both parties must agree.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Many KIT Days Can You Take?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can work for up to <strong>10 KIT days</strong> during your entire maternity leave period. Key points:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Any work done on a day counts as a full KIT day, even if you only work for one hour</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The 10 days can be used at any point during your maternity leave (except the compulsory 2-week period after birth)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You cannot carry unused KIT days over — use them or lose them</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The 10-day limit is a <strong>total</strong> across your entire leave, not per month</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Do You Get Paid for KIT Days?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">There is no statutory rate for KIT day pay — it depends on what you agree with your employer. However, most employers pay your <strong>normal daily rate</strong> for KIT days, on top of your SMP for that week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your normal salary is £30,000/year:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your daily rate is approximately £115.38</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you're in weeks 7-39, you're also receiving £194.32/week SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>For a KIT day in that period: £115.38 (KIT pay) + £194.32/7 (daily SMP) = approximately <strong>£141.68 for that day</strong>, with SMP continuing as normal</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Some employers offset the KIT day pay against your SMP, while others pay it on top. Check with your HR department before agreeing to KIT days.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Can You Do on a KIT Day?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">KIT days can be used for almost any work-related activity:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attending team meetings or away days</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Training courses or professional development</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Catching up on key projects or changes</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Handover meetings before or after your leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Conferences or networking events</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Performance reviews or appraisals</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The flexibility means you can choose activities that are genuinely useful for your career development and staying connected.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Rules and Restrictions</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">The 2-Week Compulsory Leave</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You <strong>cannot</strong> work during the first 2 weeks after your baby is born (4 weeks if you work in a factory). This is compulsory maternity leave and KIT days cannot override it.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Impact on SMP</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Working a KIT day does <strong>not</strong> stop your SMP. Your statutory maternity pay continues to be paid as normal throughout the 39-week paid period, regardless of KIT days taken.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, if you work more than 10 days during maternity leave, you will lose your SMP for each additional day worked. This is an important distinction — <strong>days 1-10 are fine, but day 11 triggers SMP loss</strong> for that week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Tax and National Insurance</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">KIT day pay is treated as normal earnings and subject to Income Tax and National Insurance deductions through PAYE, just like your regular salary.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">KIT Days vs SPLIT Days</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're on <a href="/guides/shared-parental-leave-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Shared Parental Leave</a> instead of maternity leave, you get <strong>Shared Parental Leave In Touch (SPLIT) days</strong> rather than KIT days. Each parent on SPL can work up to <strong>20 SPLIT days</strong> — offering even more flexibility.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Arrange KIT Days</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Discuss with your manager</strong> before your maternity leave begins — agree in principle which activities you might come in for</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Put agreements in writing</strong> — confirm the dates, activities, and agreed pay rate</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Keep a record</strong> — note down each KIT day used so you don't exceed 10</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Claim expenses</strong> — if you incur travel or childcare costs for a KIT day, discuss reimbursement with your employer</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Should You Use KIT Days?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">There are pros and cons to consider:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Benefits:</strong>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Stay up to date with workplace changes</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Maintain professional relationships</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Ease the anxiety of <a href="/guides/returning-to-work-after-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">returning to work</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Earn extra income on top of <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">SMP</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attend training that supports your career</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Considerations:</strong>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Arranging <a href="/guides/childcare-costs-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">childcare</a> for a single day can be difficult</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>It can feel disruptive to your maternity leave routine</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You may feel pressure to be available when you should be resting</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Travel costs may reduce the financial benefit</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">KIT Days and Your Return to Work</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many women use KIT days strategically in the weeks before their planned return:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attend a team meeting to reconnect with colleagues</span></li>
