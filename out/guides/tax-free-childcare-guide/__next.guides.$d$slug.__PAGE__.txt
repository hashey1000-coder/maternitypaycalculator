<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is Tax-Free Childcare?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Tax-Free Childcare is a government scheme that helps working parents pay for childcare. For every £8 you pay into your childcare account, the government adds £2 — meaning you get <strong>20% off your childcare costs</strong>, up to a maximum of <strong>£2,000 per child per year</strong> (or <strong>£4,000</strong> for disabled children).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This can save you thousands on nurseries, childminders, after-school clubs, and holiday clubs.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Tax-Free Childcare Works</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Open a Tax-Free Childcare account</strong> through the government's Childcare Choices website</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Pay money into the account</strong> — for every £8 you add, the government tops up £2</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Pay your childcare provider</strong> directly from the account</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>The maximum government contribution is <strong>£500 per quarter</strong> (£2,000 per year) per child</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your monthly nursery fees are £1,000:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You pay <strong>£800</strong> into your account</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The government adds <strong>£200</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You save <strong>£2,400 per year</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Can Use Tax-Free Childcare?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Both parents (or the sole parent in a single-parent family) must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Be working</strong> (employed or self-employed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Earn at least</strong> the National Minimum Wage for 16 hours per week (about £8,580/year for those aged 21+)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Earn no more than</strong> £100,000 per year each</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Not be receiving</strong> Universal Credit, Tax Credits, or childcare vouchers</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your child must be <strong>under 12</strong> (or under 17 if disabled).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">During Maternity Leave</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> You can still be eligible for Tax-Free Childcare while on maternity leave, as long as the other parent is working and meets the criteria. The parent on maternity leave is treated as meeting the work requirement for the first 31 weeks.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Free Childcare Hours</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">In addition to Tax-Free Childcare, the government provides free childcare hours:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">All 3-4 Year Olds</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>15 hours per week</strong> (570 hours per year) of free early education</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Universal — available to all families, regardless of income</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Term-time only (38 weeks)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Working Parents of 3-4 Year Olds</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>30 hours per week</strong> (1,140 hours per year) of free childcare</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Both parents must be working and earning at least the NMW for 16 hours/week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Neither parent can earn over £100,000</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Term-time only (or fewer hours spread across more weeks)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">2 Year Olds</h3>

Here's how they compare:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Tax-Free Childcare</strong> is still available and offers savings of up to <strong>£2,000 per child per year</strong>. It works for multiple children (the limit applies per child), is available to self-employed parents, and each parent must earn under <strong>£100,000</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Childcare Vouchers</strong> are closed to new joiners but existing members can continue. Savings are up to <strong>£933 per year</strong> for basic-rate taxpayers, with the same limit applying across all children. The scheme is not available to the self-employed, but there is no upper income limit.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're still on Childcare Vouchers, check whether switching to Tax-Free Childcare would save you more — particularly if you have more than one child in childcare. You need to pay the costs upfront and claim them back, and both parents must be working (or have an accepted reason for not working).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Tax-Free Childcare</strong> tops up your payments by 20%, giving you up to £2,000/year per child (£4,000 for disabled children). You pay into an online account and the government adds the top-up automatically.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For most families on Universal Credit, the <strong>Universal Credit childcare element is more generous</strong> because it covers a higher proportion of costs.
