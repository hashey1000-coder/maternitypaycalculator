<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is Statutory Adoption Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Statutory Adoption Leave is the legal right for eligible employees to take up to <strong>52 weeks off work</strong> when they adopt a child. It mirrors maternity leave in structure and provides similar pay through <strong>Statutory Adoption Pay (SAP)</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Only <strong>one parent</strong> in a couple can take adoption leave — the other parent may be eligible for paternity leave or shared parental leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Qualifies for Adoption Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for statutory adoption leave, you must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Be an <strong>employee</strong> (not a worker or self-employed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Have been <strong>matched with a child</strong> for adoption by an approved adoption agency</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Have given your employer <strong>proper notice</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> Adoption leave is a <strong>day-one right</strong> — you don't need any minimum length of service to take the leave itself. However, you do need qualifying service for the pay (see below).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Who Is Covered?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Adoption leave applies to:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Domestic adoptions through a UK adoption agency</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Overseas adoptions (with some different rules)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Foster-to-adopt placements (from when the child is placed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Surrogacy arrangements (if you obtain a parental order)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Is Statutory Adoption Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Statutory Adoption Pay (SAP) is paid for <strong>39 weeks</strong> at exactly the same rates as SMP:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>First 6 weeks</strong>: 90% of your average weekly earnings (no cap)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Remaining 33 weeks</strong>: £194.32 per week (2026/27) or 90% of AWE if lower</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Eligibility for SAP</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for the pay, you need:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>26 weeks' continuous employment</strong> with the same employer by the week you're matched with a child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Average weekly earnings</strong> of at least £129 (the Lower Earnings Limit)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't qualify for SAP, you may be eligible for Maternity Allowance — contact the DWP for advice.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Can Adoption Leave Start?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>UK adoption</strong>: Leave can start from <strong>14 days before the expected placement date</strong> or from the <strong>date of placement</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Overseas adoption</strong>: Leave can start from the date the child enters the UK or up to 28 days before the expected date of entry</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You choose when your leave starts, as long as it's within these windows.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim Adoption Leave and Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 1: Notify Your Employer</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Within <strong>7 days</strong> of being matched with a child, tell your employer:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>That you've been matched</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The expected date of placement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>When you want your adoption leave to start</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 2: Provide Evidence</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer can ask for proof of the adoption, such as:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A <strong>matching certificate</strong> from the adoption agency</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The date of placement (or expected placement)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 3: Receive Confirmation</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer must confirm your adoption leave and pay entitlement within <strong>28 days</strong> of your notification.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Adoption Leave vs Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Adoption leave and <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave</a> are very similar in structure. Both provide <strong>52 weeks of total leave</strong> with <strong>39 paid weeks</strong>. The pay rates are identical — <strong>90% of your average weekly earnings</strong> for the first 6 weeks, then <strong>£194.32/week</strong> (or 90% of AWE if lower) for the remaining 33 weeks. Both require <strong>26 weeks' qualifying service</strong> for pay (though leave itself is a day-one right), and both offer <strong>10 Keeping in Touch (KIT) days</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The main differences are:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>There is <strong>no compulsory adoption leave</strong> — you can return to work at any time</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Adoption leave can start <strong>before the placement</strong> (up to 14 days early)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Only one parent can take adoption leave — the other gets paternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Shared Parental Leave for Adoptive Parents</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Adoptive parents can use <a href="/calculators/shared-parental-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Shared Parental Leave</a> in the same way as birth parents. The primary adopter can curtail their adoption leave to create shared leave that both parents can take.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This means you can:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Share up to <strong>50 weeks of leave</strong> and <strong>37 weeks of pay</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Take leave at the same time or separately</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Take leave in blocks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Adoption and Workplace Rights</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">During adoption leave, your rights are the same as during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your <strong>employment contract continues</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You <strong>accrue annual leave</strong> as normal</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employer pension contributions</strong> continue at your full salary level during paid leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're <strong>protected from unfair dismissal</strong> and detriment</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You have the <strong>right to return</strong> to the same job (if 26 weeks or less) or a suitable alternative</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Planning for Adoption</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our calculators to plan your finances:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> — calculates SAP at the same rates as SMP</span></li>
