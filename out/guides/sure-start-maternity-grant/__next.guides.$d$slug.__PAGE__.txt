<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is the Sure Start Maternity Grant?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The Sure Start Maternity Grant is a <strong>one-off payment of £500</strong> from the UK government to help with the costs of having a new baby. It's designed to help families on low incomes buy essential items like a pushchair, cot, nappies, and clothing.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The grant is tax-free and doesn't need to be repaid — it's yours to keep.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Qualifies?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To claim the Sure Start Maternity Grant, you must meet <strong>all</strong> of these conditions:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">1. You're Receiving a Qualifying Benefit</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You (or your partner) must be receiving one of the following:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Income Support</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pension Credit</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Child Tax Credit</strong> at a rate higher than the family element</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Working Tax Credit</strong> that includes a disability or severe disability element</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">2. It's Your First Child (Usually)</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The grant is normally only available for your <strong>first child</strong>. However, you can claim for subsequent children if:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're expecting a multiple birth (twins, triplets, etc.) — you get <strong>£500 per baby</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You don't have any children under 16 currently in your household (e.g., if your previous children are now 16+)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">3. You've Had Health Professional Advice</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You must have received advice about the health and welfare of the baby from a health professional (such as a midwife, health visitor, or GP). This advice is normally given at your routine antenatal appointments.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 1: Get the SF100 Form</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Download form SF100 (Sure Start Maternity Grant) from <a href="https://www.gov.uk/sure-start-maternity-grant" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">GOV.UK</a> or request a copy by calling 0800 169 0140.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 2: Complete the Form</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You'll need to provide:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your personal details and National Insurance number</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Details of the qualifying benefit you're receiving</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Confirmation from your midwife, health visitor, or doctor (there's a section on the form for this)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 3: Submit Your Claim</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Send the completed form to the address on the form. The claim window is:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>From 11 weeks before your due date</strong> (around week 29 of pregnancy)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Up to 6 months after the birth</strong> (or adoption)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> Don't miss the deadline — claims received after 6 months from the birth cannot be accepted.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Will You Receive?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Single baby:</strong> £500</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Twins:</strong> £1,000 (£500 per baby)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Triplets:</strong> £1,500 (£500 per baby)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The money is paid directly into your bank account, usually within a few weeks of your claim being processed.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sure Start in Scotland</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you live in <strong>Scotland</strong>, the Sure Start Maternity Grant has been replaced by the <strong>Best Start Grant Pregnancy and Baby Payment</strong>:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>First child:</strong> £707.25</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Subsequent children:</strong> £353.65</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Claimed through Social Security Scotland</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sure Start in Northern Ireland</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Northern Ireland has its own <strong>Sure Start Maternity Grant</strong> with similar rules to England and Wales.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Other Financial Help for New Parents</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The Sure Start Maternity Grant is just one source of support. You may also be entitled to:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a></strong> — £27.05/week for your first child, rising to £17.90/week for subsequent children. Use our <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit Calculator</a> to see your total entitlement including any High Income Child Benefit Charge</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay</a></strong> or <strong><a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a></strong> — up to 39 weeks of pay during your maternity leave. SMP is £194.32/week at the flat rate, and Maternity Allowance can be up to the same amount</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong> — if you're on a low income, you may receive additional payments once your baby is born, including a child element and possible housing support</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Healthy Start vouchers</strong> — free food vouchers worth £4.25/week for pregnant women and £8.50/week for children under 1 on qualifying benefits, to spend on milk, fruit, vegetables, and vitamins</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Free NHS prescriptions</strong> — all pregnant women and new mothers (up to 12 months after birth) get free prescriptions. Ask your midwife for a maternity exemption certificate</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Free NHS dental care</strong> — during pregnancy and for 12 months after birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/guides/tax-free-childcare-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Tax-Free Childcare</a></strong> — saves up to £2,000/year per child on nursery and childminder costs once you return to work</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Warm Home Discount</strong> — if you're on a low income, you may qualify for £150 off your electricity bill during winter</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Getting a Maternity Exemption Certificate for Free Prescriptions</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To claim your free prescriptions, ask your midwife or GP for a maternity exemption certificate (form FW8). This is issued free of charge and entitles you to free NHS prescriptions and dental care from the start of your pregnancy until 12 months after your due date — even if your baby arrives early or late.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How to Maximise Your Financial Support</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Planning ahead can make a significant difference to your finances.
