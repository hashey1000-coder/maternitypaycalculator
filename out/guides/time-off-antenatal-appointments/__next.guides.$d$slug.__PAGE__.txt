<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Right to Paid Time Off for Antenatal Care</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">As a pregnant employee, you have a <strong>statutory right to paid time off</strong> for antenatal care. This is a day-one right — you don't need any minimum length of service.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Under the Employment Rights Act 1996, your employer <strong>must</strong> allow you reasonable time off to attend antenatal appointments and <strong>must not</strong> reduce your pay for doing so.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Counts as Antenatal Care?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Antenatal care covers a broad range of medical and health-related appointments recommended by your midwife or doctor as part of your pregnancy care:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Midwife appointments</strong> — your booking appointment (usually around 8-10 weeks), and all routine antenatal check-ups throughout pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>NHS scans</strong> — the dating scan (around 12 weeks), the anomaly scan (around 20 weeks, when you can also collect your <a href="/guides/matb1-form-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">MATB1 form</a>), and any additional growth scans</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Hospital consultant appointments</strong> — if you're under consultant-led care for a high-risk pregnancy or any pregnancy complications</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Blood tests and screening</strong> — including routine blood tests, glucose tolerance tests for gestational diabetes, and screening for conditions such as pre-eclampsia</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Antenatal classes</strong> — NHS antenatal classes and parentcraft classes, provided they are recommended by your midwife or doctor</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Mental health appointments</strong> — counselling or specialist perinatal mental health support recommended as part of your antenatal care</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Physiotherapy</strong> — if referred for pregnancy-related conditions such as pelvic girdle pain or back pain</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Relaxation and parent craft classes</strong> recommended by a registered medical practitioner</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The key test is whether the appointment is <strong>recommended by a midwife, doctor, or other registered health professional</strong> as part of your pregnancy care. If it is, your employer must allow you paid time off to attend.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">What Might Not Be Covered</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Private antenatal classes that aren't recommended by your midwife or doctor</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Appointments for your partner (see partner rights below)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Baby shopping or nursery preparation</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>General lifestyle activities such as pregnancy yoga (unless specifically recommended by a health professional)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Travel Time</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your entitlement includes <strong>reasonable travel time</strong> to and from the appointment. If your appointment requires a long journey — for example, to a specialist hospital — your employer must allow the full time needed, not just the appointment itself.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Time Off Can You Take?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">There's no statutory limit on the number of appointments you can attend. You're entitled to <strong>reasonable</strong> paid time off for <strong>every</strong> antenatal appointment. This includes the time travelling to and from appointments.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Do You Need to Show Proof?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>For your <strong>first appointment</strong>: your employer cannot ask for proof</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>For <strong>subsequent appointments</strong>: your employer can ask to see your appointment card or a letter confirming the appointment</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer <strong>cannot</strong> insist on seeing your medical records</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights in Practice</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer must:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Pay you</strong> your normal rate of pay for time off for antenatal appointments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Not insist</strong> that you make up the time or take annual leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Not move you</strong> to different shifts to avoid antenatal appointments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Not treat you unfavourably</strong> for attending appointments</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer <strong>cannot</strong>:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Refuse you time off for antenatal appointments</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Require you to rearrange appointments outside working hours</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Dock your pay or require you to use holiday</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Discipline you for attending appointments</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Partners' Rights to Antenatal Appointments</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Partners (including same-sex partners, civil partners, and fathers) have <strong>separate rights</strong>:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You can take <strong>unpaid</strong> time off to attend <strong>up to 2 antenatal appointments</strong> with the expectant mother</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Each appointment can last up to <strong>6.5 hours</strong> (including travel time)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This right applies to employees and agency workers</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Note: This right is to <strong>unpaid</strong> leave. Some employers voluntarily offer paid time off for partners to attend appointments — check your company's policy.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Agency Workers and Antenatal Appointments</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Agency workers have the same right to paid time off for antenatal appointments as employees, <strong>after 12 weeks in the same placement</strong>. Before this qualifying period, you may need to rearrange work hours or take unpaid time.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If Your Employer Refuses?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer refuses to allow you time off for antenatal care:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Put your request in writing</strong> citing your statutory right under the Employment Rights Act 1996</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Contact ACAS</strong> for free advice (0300 123 1100)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>You may be able to bring a claim to an <strong>Employment Tribunal</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Refusing antenatal time off may also constitute <a href="/guides/pregnancy-discrimination-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy discrimination</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tips for Managing Antenatal Appointments at Work</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Give your employer as much notice as possible</strong> — while they can't refuse, being considerate helps maintain a good relationship</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Try to schedule appointments</strong> at the start or end of the day where possible (but you're not obligated to)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Keep your appointment card</strong> handy in case your employer asks to see it</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Know your rights</strong> — if you feel pressured to skip appointments, that could constitute <a href="/guides/pregnancy-discrimination-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy discrimination</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Put requests in writing</strong> — if your employer is awkward about time off, email your request so there's a paper trail</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Remember it's paid time</strong> — your employer cannot ask you to make up the hours, use annual leave, or take unpaid time</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Antenatal Time Off Relates to Your Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your antenatal appointments are separate from your <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-rights/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity leave entitlement</a>. They don't reduce your maternity leave or affect your <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay</a> calculation. Once your maternity leave begins, antenatal appointment rights no longer apply — but by then, your baby should have arrived.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're still working in the later stages of pregnancy, you may find appointments become more frequent — typically fortnightly from around 28 weeks, and weekly from 36 weeks. Your employer must accommodate all of these, even if it means significant time away from work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to work out your key dates, and our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see how your SMP will be calculated.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Related Guides</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/guides/pregnancy-rights-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Pregnancy Rights at Work</a> — your full legal protections</span></li>
