<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Can You Resign During Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Yes — you have every right to resign from your job at any time during maternity leave. You don't need to give a reason, and your employer cannot penalise you for making this decision.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, there are important financial and practical implications you should understand before handing in your notice.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Happens to Your SMP If You Resign?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is the most common concern, and the answer is reassuring:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>You do NOT lose your SMP if you resign.</strong> Statutory Maternity Pay continues for the full 39 weeks regardless of whether you resign during maternity leave. SMP is a statutory entitlement — once you've qualified for it, you're entitled to it even if your employment ends.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Key Points:</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>SMP is payable for up to 39 weeks from the date it started</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must continue paying your SMP even after you resign</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>SMP continues through your notice period and beyond</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your resignation does not affect the amount of SMP you receive</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Notice Period</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When you resign during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must give the <strong>contractual notice</strong> required in your employment contract (or statutory minimum of 1 week if your contract doesn't specify)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your notice period <strong>runs concurrently</strong> with your maternity leave — you don't need to return to work during your notice</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must continue paying your SMP throughout the notice period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're not entitled to normal salary during your notice if you're still on maternity leave (SMP continues instead)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're on week 20 of SMP and give 4 weeks' notice:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks 20-24: You receive SMP as normal (you're on maternity leave and notice simultaneously)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Week 24: Your employment ends</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks 25-39: Your employer continues paying SMP until it finishes</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Do You Have to Repay Enhanced Maternity Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This depends entirely on your employer's maternity policy:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Statutory SMP</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>No — you never have to repay SMP.</strong> This is your statutory right regardless of whether you return to work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Enhanced (Occupational) Maternity Pay</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Possibly.</strong> Many employers include a <strong>clawback clause</strong> in their maternity policy requiring you to repay some or all of the enhanced portion if you don't return to work for a specified period (usually 3-6 months).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Before resigning, check:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employment contract</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer's maternity policy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Any separate agreement you signed about enhanced pay</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If there is a clawback clause, you'll need to repay the <strong>enhanced portion only</strong> (the amount above SMP). You can never be required to repay the statutory SMP element.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/enhanced-maternity-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Enhanced Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see the difference between your enhanced pay and SMP.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Happens to Your Annual Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><a href="/guides/annual-leave-during-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Annual leave</a> <strong>continues to accrue</strong> throughout your maternity leave (both paid and unpaid weeks). When you resign:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Any untaken annual leave must be <strong>paid out</strong> in your final pay</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This can be a significant sum — you may have accrued 5-6 weeks of holiday</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Check with HR for your exact accrued leave balance</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Calculation Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you accrue 28 days annual leave per year and take 52 weeks maternity leave:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You accrue <strong>28 days</strong> during maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Plus any leave from before your maternity leave started</span></li>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Factor in annual leave payout</strong> — this could be paid as a lump sum</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Check clawback clauses</strong> — sometimes waiting a few extra months avoids repayment</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">New Job Timing</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Some women resign to start a new job — you can start a new job during maternity leave while still receiving SMP from your previous employer</span></li>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Working for a new employer during your SMP period ends your SMP entitlement with the old employer (unless it's KIT days)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Notice Period</h3>

<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Consider whether you want any accrued leave paid out or taken as time off</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Other Options to Consider</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Before deciding to resign, think about:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/guides/flexible-working-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Flexible working request</a></strong> — your employer must consider it</span></li>
