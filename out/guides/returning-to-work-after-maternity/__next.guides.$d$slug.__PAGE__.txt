<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Planning Your Return</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Returning to work after maternity leave is a big transition. Here's everything you need to consider to make it as smooth as possible.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">When to Return</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You don't need to give notice if you're returning after your full 52 weeks. But if you want to return <strong>earlier</strong>, you must give your employer at least <strong>8 weeks' notice</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Your Right to Return</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>After Ordinary Maternity Leave (first 26 weeks)</strong>: You must be allowed back to your <strong>exact same job</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>After Additional Maternity Leave (last 26 weeks)</strong>: Same job, or a suitable alternative on equal terms</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Flexible Working</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Since 2024, every employee in the UK has the right to request flexible working from <strong>day one</strong> of employment. This is particularly valuable when returning from maternity leave, as it helps you balance work with your new caring responsibilities.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Types of Flexible Working</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Part-time hours</strong>: Reducing your weekly hours, for example from five days to three or four</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Compressed hours</strong>: Working your full-time hours over fewer days (e.g., four longer days instead of five)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Flexitime</strong>: Choosing when you start and finish, which can help with nursery drop-offs and pick-ups</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Job sharing</strong>: Splitting a full-time role with another person</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Working from home</strong>: Remote or hybrid arrangements, reducing commuting time and costs</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Term-time working</strong>: Only working during school terms (where the employer agrees)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How to Make a Flexible Working Request</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can make up to two statutory flexible working requests per year. To give yourself the best chance of success:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span>Put your request in writing, explaining the pattern you want and how it could work for your team</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span>Consider the business impact and suggest solutions to any potential issues</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Be open to compromise — your employer may propose an alternative arrangement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span>Your employer must respond within <strong>2 months</strong> and can only refuse for one of eight specific business reasons set out in law</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your request is refused, you can appeal the decision. Read our full guide on <a href="/guides/flexible-working-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">flexible working after maternity leave</a> for detailed advice on making a successful request.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights if Your Role Has Changed</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">When you return from maternity leave, you have a legal right to return to your job. However, you may find that things have changed during your absence. Here is what you need to know:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>After Ordinary Maternity Leave</strong> (first 26 weeks): You must be offered the <strong>exact same job</strong> on the same terms and conditions</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>After Additional Maternity Leave</strong> (last 26 weeks): You should be offered the same job, or if that is not reasonably practicable, a suitable alternative on terms no less favourable</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer has restructured your team or changed your role significantly without consulting you, this could amount to <a href="/guides/pregnancy-discrimination-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy discrimination</a>. Keep a record of any changes and seek advice from ACAS if you are unsure about your rights.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your role has been made redundant during your maternity leave, you have <a href="/guides/redundancy-during-pregnancy-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">enhanced redundancy protection</a> and must be offered any suitable alternative vacancy ahead of other employees. This protection now extends to 18 months after the birth of your child.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Planning for Your Return</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Returning to work brings new financial considerations. Take time to review your household budget and plan for the transition:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Calculate your return-to-work salary</strong> — if you are switching to part-time hours, use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to see your new after-tax income</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Factor in childcare costs</strong> — childcare is likely your biggest new expense. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/childcare-costs-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">childcare costs after maternity leave</a> to understand your options</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Claim your annual leave</strong> — you will have accrued <a href="/guides/annual-leave-during-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">annual leave during maternity</a>, which could mean extra paid days off or a lump-sum payout</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Check your pension</strong> — your employer should have continued <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-pension-contributions/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pension contributions during your paid maternity leave</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Review your tax code</strong> — your tax code may need updating after a period of lower income</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For a full financial overview, read our guide on <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">financial planning for maternity leave</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Childcare Costs and Support</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Tax-Free Childcare</h3>
The government pays £2 for every £8 you pay into a childcare account, up to £2,000 per year per child (£4,000 for disabled children). Read our <a href="/guides/tax-free-childcare-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Tax-Free Childcare guide</a> for full details on how to apply.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">30 Hours Free Childcare</h3>
Working parents of 3–4 year olds can get 30 hours of free childcare per week. From 2025, this was extended to children from 9 months old.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Childcare Vouchers</h3>
While the scheme is closed to new applicants, existing users can continue using salary sacrifice childcare vouchers.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Universal Credit Childcare</h3>
If you are on Universal Credit, you can claim up to 85% of your childcare costs. See our guide on <a href="/guides/benefits-during-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">benefits during maternity leave</a> for more details.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">KIT Days Before Returning</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use your remaining <a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Keeping in Touch (KIT) days</a> wisely:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Attend a team catch-up a few weeks before your return</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Complete any mandatory training</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Discuss your role and any changes with your manager</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Sort out practical arrangements (desk, IT access, parking)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Breastfeeding at Work</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer must:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Carry out a risk assessment for breastfeeding employees</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Provide a <strong>private room</strong> (not a toilet) for expressing milk</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Allow reasonable breaks for expressing</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Provide access to a fridge for storing milk</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Emotional Wellbeing</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">It is completely normal to feel a range of emotions about returning to work after maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Guilt</strong> about leaving your baby</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Anxiety</strong> about managing work and parenthood</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Excitement</strong> about returning to your career</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Worry</strong> about catching up on changes</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Consider speaking to your GP if you are feeling particularly anxious or low. Many employers also offer employee assistance programmes with free, confidential counselling.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Checklist for Returning to Work</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>[ ] Give 8 weeks' notice if returning early</span></li>
