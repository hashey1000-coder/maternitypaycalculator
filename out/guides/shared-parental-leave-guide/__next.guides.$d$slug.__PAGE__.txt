<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What is Shared Parental Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Shared Parental Leave (SPL) lets eligible parents share up to <strong>50 weeks of leave</strong> and <strong>37 weeks of pay</strong> between them. It gives families more flexibility in how they care for their child in the first year.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Does SPL Work?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The mother must "curtail" (end early) her maternity leave and convert the remaining entitlement into SPL. For example:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If the mother takes 20 weeks of maternity leave, up to <strong>32 weeks</strong> of leave and up to <strong>19 weeks of pay</strong> can be shared</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Both parents can take leave at the <strong>same time</strong> or <strong>separately</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Leave can be taken in up to <strong>3 separate blocks</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Eligibility</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">For the Mother/Primary Adopter</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Must be eligible for maternity leave or Maternity Allowance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Must share the main responsibility for the child with the partner</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">For the Partner</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Must have been employed for at least 26 weeks by the 15th week before the due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Must have earned at least £390 in total in 13 of the 66 weeks before the due date</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Both Must</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Share the main responsibility for the child</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Give their employers the correct notice</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Shared Parental Leave Pay (ShPP)</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">ShPP is paid at the same rate as standard SMP:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£194.32 per week</strong> (or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The total number of ShPP weeks depends on how many weeks of SMP/MA the mother has already used</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Note:</strong> The first 6 weeks of enhanced maternity pay (90% of earnings) cannot be shared — this always belongs to the mother.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Apply</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 1: Mother Gives Notice to End Maternity Leave</h3>
Submit a "curtailment notice" to her employer, stating when maternity leave will end.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 2: Both Parents Submit Notices</h3>
Both parents must give their employers a "notice of entitlement" at least <strong>8 weeks</strong> before any SPL starts.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 3: Book Leave</h3>
Submit a "period of leave notice" for each block of leave, at least 8 weeks in advance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">SPL Pay Examples</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To help you understand the financial impact of Shared Parental Leave, here are some practical examples of how ShPP works:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Example 1: Mother takes 20 weeks, partner takes 19 weeks</strong>
The mother takes 20 weeks of maternity leave (6 weeks at 90% of her average weekly earnings, then 14 weeks at £194.32/week). She then curtails her leave, and 19 weeks of ShPP remain. Her partner takes 19 weeks of SPL, receiving £194.32 per week (or 90% of their average weekly earnings, whichever is lower).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Example 2: Both parents off together for 4 weeks</strong>
The mother takes 30 weeks of maternity leave, then curtails. She and her partner both take 4 weeks of SPL at the same time, followed by the partner taking another 5 weeks alone. The mother uses 4 weeks of ShPP and the partner uses 9 weeks — totalling 13 weeks of shared pay from the remaining entitlement.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Example 3: Leave in blocks</strong>
The mother takes 26 weeks of maternity leave, returns to work for 8 weeks, then takes another 6 weeks of SPL. Her partner takes 7 weeks of SPL while the mother is back at work. This kind of flexibility — taking leave in separate blocks — is only available through SPL, not standard maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see your week-by-week pay breakdown and understand how curtailing maternity leave affects your total entitlement. You can also use our <a href="/calculators/shared-parental-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Shared Parental Leave Calculator</a> to model different scenarios for your family.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Enhanced Shared Parental Leave Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Some employers offer <strong>enhanced SPL pay</strong> that goes above the statutory rate of £194.32 per week. This could mean full salary for a set number of weeks during SPL, similar to <a href="/calculators/enhanced-maternity-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">enhanced maternity pay</a> schemes.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, it is worth noting that not all employers who offer enhanced maternity pay extend the same benefits to SPL. Check your employer's policy carefully — you can find details in your staff handbook or by speaking to your HR department. If your employer only enhances maternity pay but not SPL pay, you will want to factor this into your decision.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to compare your household income under different leave-sharing scenarios and see which arrangement works best financially for your family.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Practical Considerations</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Is SPL Right for Your Family?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Deciding whether to use Shared Parental Leave requires careful consideration of several factors:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Financial impact</strong>: Will the higher earner be taking time off at the statutory rate? Calculate whether your household can manage on £194.32 per week for the weeks your partner takes SPL. Use our <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">financial planning guide</a> to budget effectively.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employer policies</strong>: Check both employers' SPL policies. Some offer enhanced pay, while others only provide the statutory minimum.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Childcare arrangements</strong>: SPL can help you stagger your leave so one parent is always at home, potentially reducing <a href="/guides/childcare-costs-after-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">childcare costs</a> in the early months.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Career considerations</strong>: Consider how each parent's career might be affected and discuss this openly together.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Employer attitudes</strong>: While employers cannot refuse SPL, their level of support and flexibility around discontinuous leave patterns may vary.</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How SPL Compares with Maternity Leave</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">SPL offers greater flexibility than standard maternity leave. With maternity leave, you must take your leave in one continuous block. With SPL, you can take leave in up to <strong>three separate blocks</strong>, and both parents can be off at the same time.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, the first 6 weeks of enhanced pay (90% of the mother's average weekly earnings) cannot be shared — this always remains with the mother as part of her compulsory maternity leave. Only the remaining weeks at the flat rate of £194.32 per week can be converted to ShPP.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Each parent also gets <strong>20 Shared Parental Leave in Touch (SPLIT) days</strong>, compared with 10 <a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">KIT days</a> during standard maternity leave. This means both parents can keep in contact with their employers more easily during SPL.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Common Questions</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Can my employer refuse SPL?</strong>
Your employer cannot refuse your statutory entitlement to Shared Parental Leave. However, they can refuse a "discontinuous" leave pattern (where you request leave in separate blocks with gaps between them). If they refuse a discontinuous pattern, you can instead take the same total amount of leave as one continuous block.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Can I change my mind?</strong>
You can revoke the curtailment notice within 8 weeks of giving it, in certain circumstances — for example, if you gave notice before the birth and the baby has not yet been born.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>How does SPL affect my pension?</strong>
The rules are the same as for maternity leave during the paid period. Your employer must continue making <a href="/guides/maternity-leave-pension-contributions/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pension contributions</a> based on your normal salary during any weeks you receive ShPP. Check with your employer for the specifics of your scheme.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Can I use SPL if I'm not the birth mother?</strong>
Yes. SPL is available to both parents, including the partner of the birth mother and adoptive parents. For adoption-specific guidance, read our <a href="/guides/adoption-leave-and-pay-uk/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">adoption leave and pay guide</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>What if one parent is self-employed?</strong>
Self-employed parents cannot claim ShPP directly. However, if the mother claims <a href="/guides/maternity-allowance-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> rather than SMP, the employed partner may still be eligible for SPL provided they meet the employment and earnings criteria.
