<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Pay When You Change Jobs</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Changing jobs while pregnant — whether by choice or circumstance — raises important questions about your maternity pay entitlement. The answer depends on when you changed jobs relative to your due date and how long you've been with your new employer.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The 26-Week Employment Rule</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)</a>, you must have been continuously employed by the <strong>same employer</strong> for at least 26 weeks by the end of the qualifying week (the 15th week before your due date).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This means:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you change jobs <strong>after</strong> the qualifying week: You may still qualify with your original employer (if you're still employed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you change jobs <strong>before</strong> completing 26 weeks with the new employer: You won't qualify for SMP from them</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you're between jobs: You won't qualify for SMP from either employer</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Calculating Your Qualifying Date</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your qualifying week is approximately <strong>25 weeks before your due date</strong>. To qualify for SMP, you need 26 weeks of service by this point. That means you need to have started your job no later than approximately <strong>51 weeks before your due date</strong> — nearly a year.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to calculate your exact qualifying week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Allowance — Your Safety Net</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't qualify for SMP because you've changed jobs, <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance (MA)</a> is your primary alternative. The great news is that MA has much more flexible eligibility:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You need to have been employed or self-employed for at least <strong>26 weeks in the 66 weeks</strong> before your due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>These 26 weeks can be with <strong>any employer</strong> — they don't need to be with the same one</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You need to have earned at least <strong>£30 per week</strong> in any 13 of those weeks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This means that even if you've changed jobs multiple times, you're likely to qualify for MA.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">MA Rate</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The maximum rate is <strong>£194.32 per week</strong> for 39 weeks (the same flat rate as SMP), and critically, <strong>Maternity Allowance is completely tax-free</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Scenarios Explained</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Scenario 1: Changed Jobs Midway Through Pregnancy</h3>
You were employed at Company A for 3 years, left, and started at Company B at 20 weeks pregnant.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP from Company B?</strong> No — you won't have 26 weeks' service by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP from Company A?</strong> No — you're no longer employed there</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Maternity Allowance?</strong> Yes — you've been employed for well over 26 weeks in the 66-week window</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Your entitlement</strong>: MA at up to £194.32/week for 39 weeks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Scenario 2: Started New Job Just Before Getting Pregnant</h3>
You started a new job in January and found out you were pregnant in March, with a due date in December.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your qualifying week is approximately September</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>By September, you'll have about 35 weeks of service — more than 26 weeks</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>SMP from your employer?</strong> Yes — as long as you earn at least £129/week</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Scenario 3: Made Redundant While Pregnant</h3>
If you're made redundant during pregnancy:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer must offer you any suitable alternative vacancy (you have priority)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If there's no alternative, you may receive SMP if you were employed long enough before the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If not, claim Maternity Allowance</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What You Should Do</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Calculate your qualifying week</strong> using our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">date planner</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Check your service length</strong> — count back 26 weeks from the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>If you don't qualify for SMP</strong>: Ask your employer for form SMP1 (they must provide this)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Apply for Maternity Allowance</strong>: Use form MA1 from GOV.UK, including the SMP1 form</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Don't delay</strong>: Apply for MA from week 26 of pregnancy</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights as a New Employee</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Even if you've just started a new job, you have rights:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>52 weeks of maternity leave</strong> — available from day one of employment (but leave, not pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Paid time off for antenatal appointments</strong> — from day one</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Protection from dismissal</strong> — from the moment you tell your employer</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Health and safety protections</strong> — immediate</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read more about your <a href="/guides/pregnancy-rights-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy rights at work</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">TUPE Transfers</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer is taken over or your role is transferred under TUPE (Transfer of Undertakings Protection of Employment), your continuity of service is preserved. This means:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your service with the old employer counts towards the 26-week SMP qualifying period with the new employer</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your existing terms and conditions, including any enhanced maternity pay, are protected</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your new employer cannot reduce your maternity entitlement as part of the transfer</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you're pregnant during a TUPE transfer, you're additionally protected from <a href="/guides/redundancy-during-pregnancy-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">redundancy and unfair dismissal</a></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is particularly relevant for workers in outsourced services, the public sector, or during company mergers. If you're unsure whether your transfer qualifies as a TUPE transfer, seek advice from your union or ACAS.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Planning When Changing Jobs</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Changing jobs during pregnancy requires extra financial planning:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Calculate your Maternity Allowance</a> if SMP isn't available</span></li>
