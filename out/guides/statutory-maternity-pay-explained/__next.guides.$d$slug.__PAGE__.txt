<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What is Statutory Maternity Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) is the minimum amount your employer must pay you when you take maternity leave. It's paid for up to <strong>39 weeks</strong> and is funded by the UK government through your employer.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Key SMP Facts for 2026/27</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>First 6 weeks</strong>: 90% of your average weekly earnings (before tax)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Remaining 33 weeks</strong>: £194.32 per week or 90% of AWE (whichever is lower)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Total duration</strong>: 39 weeks of paid leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Additional leave</strong>: 13 weeks unpaid (52 weeks total entitlement)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Qualifies for SMP?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay, you must meet <strong>all</strong> of these conditions:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Employment</strong>: You must be employed (not self-employed) by the same employer for a continuous period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>26-week rule</strong>: You must have worked for your employer continuously for at least 26 weeks by the end of the 15th week before your expected due date (known as the "qualifying week")</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Earnings threshold</strong>: Your average weekly earnings must be at least <strong>£129</strong> (the Lower Earnings Limit for National Insurance)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Notice</strong>: You must give your employer at least 28 days' notice (or as much notice as reasonably possible)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Proof of pregnancy</strong>: You must provide medical evidence of your pregnancy (usually a MATB1 certificate from your midwife or doctor)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How is SMP Calculated?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your SMP is calculated based on your <strong>Average Weekly Earnings (AWE)</strong>. This is worked out using your pay over an 8-week reference period before the qualifying week.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Calculation Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your annual salary is <strong>£35,000</strong>:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weekly earnings = £35,000 ÷ 52 = <strong>£673.08</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>First 6 weeks (90%): <strong>£605.77 per week</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Next 33 weeks: <strong>£194.32 per week</strong> (flat rate, as 90% of AWE exceeds this)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Total SMP: (6 × £605.77) + (33 × £194.32) = <strong>£10,047.18</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to get your personalised breakdown.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Does SMP Start?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">SMP can start from <strong>11 weeks before your due date</strong> at the earliest. The latest it can start is the day after your baby is born.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You choose when to start your maternity leave, but there are some automatic triggers:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you're off work with a pregnancy-related illness in the <strong>4 weeks before your due date</strong>, your SMP starts automatically</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If your baby is born early, your SMP starts the day after the birth</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Tax and National Insurance on SMP</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">SMP is treated as earnings, which means:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You <strong>do</strong> pay Income Tax on SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You <strong>do</strong> pay National Insurance on SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer deducts these before paying you</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">However, because SMP is lower than your normal salary, you'll likely pay less tax overall. Use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to see your after-tax amount.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim SMP</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 1: Notify Your Employer</h3>
Tell your employer you're pregnant and when you want to start maternity leave. You should do this by the <strong>15th week before your due date</strong>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 2: Provide Your MATB1</h3>
Give your employer your MATB1 certificate, which you'll receive from your midwife or doctor around week 20-21 of pregnancy.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Step 3: Confirm Dates</h3>
Your employer must respond within 28 days, confirming:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>When your maternity leave starts</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>When your maternity leave ends</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your SMP entitlement</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What If You Don't Qualify for SMP?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't meet the eligibility criteria for Statutory Maternity Pay, don't worry — there are other options available to you. Your employer must provide you with form SMP1 explaining why you don't qualify, and you should keep this form as you will need it when applying for alternative support.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a></strong>: The main alternative for those who don't meet SMP criteria. You may qualify if you've been employed or self-employed for at least 26 weeks in the 66 weeks before your due date. Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance Calculator</a> to check your entitlement, or read our full <a href="/guides/maternity-allowance-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance guide</a>.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong>: You may be able to claim additional financial support during maternity leave if your household income is low. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/benefits-during-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">benefits during maternity leave</a> for more details.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/guides/sure-start-maternity-grant/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Sure Start Maternity Grant</a></strong>: A one-off payment of £500 if you're on certain benefits and expecting your first child (or a multiple birth).</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you've recently changed jobs and don't qualify for SMP with your new employer, our guide on <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-changing-jobs/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity pay when changing jobs</a> explains your options in full. You can also use our <a href="/calculators/smp-vs-maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">SMP vs Maternity Allowance comparison tool</a> to see which benefit applies to you.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">SMP and Multiple Jobs</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you work for more than one employer, you may be able to claim Statutory Maternity Pay from <strong>each employer</strong> separately, provided you meet the qualifying conditions with each one. This means:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must have been continuously employed by each employer for at least 26 weeks by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your average weekly earnings must be at least £129 (the Lower Earnings Limit) with <strong>each</strong> employer independently</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You need to give proper notice and provide your MATB1 certificate to each employer</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you only qualify with one employer, you can still claim SMP from that employer and may be able to claim <a href="/guides/maternity-allowance-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> based on your other employment. This is common for women who work <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-part-time-workers/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">part-time</a> across multiple jobs or who work through an <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-agency-zero-hours-workers/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">agency or on zero-hours contracts</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Enhanced Maternity Pay</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many employers offer enhanced maternity pay packages that go beyond the statutory minimum. This could mean full salary for a set number of weeks, or a higher rate for a longer period. Enhanced maternity pay varies widely between employers — some offer just a few weeks at full pay, while others (such as the <a href="/guides/nhs-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">NHS</a> or <a href="/guides/teacher-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">teaching sector</a>) have well-known enhanced schemes.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Check your employment contract or staff handbook to see what your employer offers. Use our <a href="/calculators/enhanced-maternity-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Enhanced Maternity Pay Calculator</a> to see how your employer's scheme compares with SMP, and read our guide on <a href="/guides/how-much-maternity-pay-will-i-get/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">how much maternity pay you'll get</a> for a full breakdown.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Employer's Responsibilities</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your employer <strong>cannot</strong> refuse to pay SMP if you meet the eligibility criteria. They can reclaim 92–103% of SMP from HMRC, so it doesn't cost them directly. Small employers may be able to reclaim 103% through the Small Employers' Relief scheme.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer disputes your eligibility, they must give you form SMP1 explaining why. You can challenge their decision through HMRC, or apply for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> from the DWP as an alternative. If you believe your employer is treating you unfairly because of your pregnancy, read our guide on <a href="/guides/pregnancy-discrimination-at-work/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">pregnancy discrimination at work</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Planning Your Maternity Leave Finances</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Understanding your SMP entitlement is the first step in <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">financial planning for maternity leave</a>.
