<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Is Neonatal Care Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Neonatal Care Leave is a <strong>new statutory entitlement</strong> introduced on <strong>6 April 2025</strong> that gives parents the right to take paid time off work when their baby requires neonatal care. This is one of the most significant new parental rights in recent years.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Previously, parents of babies in neonatal intensive care units (NICU) or special care baby units (SCBU) had no specific right to additional leave. They had to rely on maternity/paternity leave, annual leave, or unpaid leave — meaning their paid maternity leave was being used up while their baby was in hospital.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Who Qualifies for Neonatal Care Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To be eligible, you must be:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>An <strong>employee</strong> (not a worker or self-employed)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The parent (birth parent, partner, or intended parent through surrogacy/adoption) of a baby who <strong>receives neonatal care within 28 days of birth</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The baby must require neonatal care for <strong>at least 7 continuous days</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> Neonatal care leave is a <strong>day-one right</strong> — you don't need any minimum length of service with your employer.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Neonatal Care Leave Can You Take?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can take <strong>up to 12 weeks</strong> of neonatal care leave, calculated as:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>1 week of leave for each week</strong> (or part-week) your baby is in neonatal care</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Maximum of <strong>12 weeks</strong> total</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Taken in blocks of <strong>at least 1 week</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This leave is <strong>in addition to</strong> your existing maternity, paternity, adoption, or shared parental leave. It doesn't reduce your other leave entitlements.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Is Neonatal Care Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Statutory Neonatal Care Pay is paid at:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£194.32 per week</strong> (2026/27 rate) — the same as the lower rate of SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Or <strong>90% of your average weekly earnings</strong> if that's less</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for the <strong>pay</strong> element, you need:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>At least <strong>26 weeks' continuous service</strong> with your employer by the qualifying week</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Average weekly earnings of at least <strong>£129</strong> (the Lower Earnings Limit)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Even if you don't qualify for neonatal care <strong>pay</strong>, you can still take neonatal care <strong>leave</strong> (unpaid).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Does It Work Alongside Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Neonatal care leave works <strong>in addition to</strong> maternity leave. Here's how it typically works:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Birth and NICU admission</strong> — your maternity leave may have already started, or it triggers automatically</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>During neonatal care</strong> — your maternity leave continues as normal during this period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Baby discharged home</strong> — after your baby comes home, you can take neonatal care leave on top of your remaining maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Extended time at home</strong> — this means you get extra weeks with your baby at home, rather than using up maternity leave while your baby is in hospital</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example Scenario</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Sarah's baby is born prematurely and spends 8 weeks in NICU. Without neonatal care leave, Sarah would only have 44 weeks of maternity leave left at home. With the new right, Sarah can take her full 52 weeks of maternity leave <strong>plus</strong> up to 8 weeks of neonatal care leave — giving her up to 60 weeks total.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim Neonatal Care Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Notify your employer</strong> that your baby requires neonatal care</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Provide evidence</strong> if requested (e.g. a letter from the hospital)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Request leave</strong> — you can start leave immediately during the neonatal care period (no notice required), or give notice to take it later</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Claim pay</strong> — if eligible, statutory neonatal care pay is processed through your employer's payroll</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Rights During Neonatal Care Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">During neonatal care leave, you have the same protections as during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your <strong>employment contract continues</strong> — you accrue annual leave, pension rights, etc.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You're <strong>protected from dismissal</strong> related to taking neonatal care leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You have the <strong>right to return</strong> to the same job (or a suitable alternative if combined with other extended leave)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You cannot suffer any <strong>detriment</strong> for taking neonatal care leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Neonatal Care Leave for Partners</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The neonatal care leave right applies to <strong>both parents</strong>, not just the birth mother. This means:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Partners</strong> can take up to 12 weeks in addition to their paternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Both parents</strong> can take neonatal care leave at the same time</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This gives families crucial time together after a traumatic start</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Counts as "Neonatal Care"?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Neonatal care includes any medical care received by a baby in a hospital setting, including:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Neonatal intensive care unit (NICU)</span></li>
