<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What is Maternity Allowance?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Maternity Allowance (MA) is a benefit paid by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) to pregnant women who don't qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay. It's designed as a safety net for:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Self-employed</strong> women</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Women who've <strong>recently changed jobs</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Women who've <strong>stopped working</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Those who don't earn enough for SMP</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">How Does Maternity Allowance Differ from SMP?</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Maternity Allowance and <a href="/guides/statutory-maternity-pay-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)</a> are both designed to support you financially during maternity leave, but there are important differences you should understand.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Who pays:</strong> SMP is paid by your employer (who reclaims it from HMRC), while Maternity Allowance is paid directly by the DWP.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Higher-rate period:</strong> With SMP, you receive 90% of your average weekly earnings for the first 6 weeks. Maternity Allowance does not have this higher-rate period — it is paid at a flat rate throughout all 39 weeks.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Rate of pay:</strong> Both benefits pay up to £194.32 per week at the standard rate for 2026/27. With MA, you receive the lower of 90% of your average weekly earnings or £194.32 per week for the full duration.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Tax treatment:</strong> SMP is treated as earnings and is subject to Income Tax and National Insurance. Maternity Allowance is <strong>not taxable</strong>, which can be a significant advantage — you keep the full amount. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/is-maternity-pay-taxed/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">whether maternity pay is taxed</a> for more details.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/smp-vs-maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">SMP vs Maternity Allowance comparison tool</a> to see which benefit applies to your situation.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Eligibility for Maternity Allowance</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You may qualify if you've been:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Route 1: Employed or Self-Employed</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Employed or self-employed for at least <strong>26 weeks</strong> in the 66 weeks before your due date (these weeks don't need to be consecutive)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Earning at least <strong>£30 per week</strong> in any 13 of those weeks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Route 2: Helping Your Partner</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You've been helping your self-employed spouse or civil partner with their business for at least 26 weeks in the 66 weeks before your due date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You receive a lower rate of £27 per week for 14 weeks</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much is Maternity Allowance?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The standard rate is the <strong>lower of</strong>:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>90% of your average weekly earnings</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>£194.32 per week (2026/27 rate)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance Calculator</a> to work out your exact entitlement based on your earnings.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For the full 39 weeks at the maximum rate, your total Maternity Allowance would be <strong>£7,578.48</strong>. If your average weekly earnings are below £215.91, your MA will be 90% of your earnings rather than the maximum rate.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Does Maternity Allowance Start and End?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You can start receiving Maternity Allowance from the <strong>11th week before your expected due date</strong> at the earliest. The latest it can start is the day after your baby is born.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You choose your start date when you apply. Unlike SMP, there is no automatic trigger if you are off sick before your due date — your MA start date remains as you requested. Maternity Allowance is paid for a maximum of <strong>39 weeks</strong> from your chosen start date.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to work out your key dates, including the earliest date you could start receiving MA. For guidance on choosing the right time, read our guide on <a href="/guides/when-to-start-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">when to start maternity leave</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How to Claim Maternity Allowance</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Get form MA1</strong> — download it from GOV.UK or pick one up from your local Jobcentre Plus</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Gather your evidence</strong> — you will need your <a href="/guides/matb1-form-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">MATB1 certificate</a> (from your midwife or doctor, usually issued around week 20), recent payslips or proof of self-employment earnings, and your SMP1 form if your employer has confirmed you don't qualify for SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Complete the form</strong> with your employment history and earnings details for the relevant 66-week period</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Apply from week 26</strong> of your pregnancy — you can submit your claim as soon as you reach 26 weeks pregnant</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Wait for a decision</strong> — claims typically take up to 24 working days to process</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Make sure you apply early to avoid delays. If your claim is rejected, you can ask for a mandatory reconsideration.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Allowance for Self-Employed Women</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you are self-employed, Maternity Allowance is likely your main source of maternity income, as self-employed women do not qualify for SMP. To claim, you will usually need to show that you have been registered as self-employed and paying Class 2 National Insurance contributions.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your MA rate is based on your self-employed earnings. If your profits are low, you may receive less than the maximum £194.32 per week rate. Read our detailed guide on <a href="/guides/maternity-pay-self-employed/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">maternity pay for the self-employed</a> for more information on how your earnings are assessed and what evidence you need to provide.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Allowance and Other Benefits</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Maternity Allowance is <strong>not taxable</strong>, which is a significant advantage over SMP. However, it may affect your entitlement to other means-tested benefits:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Universal Credit</strong> — MA is counted as income, which may reduce your UC payments. See our guide on <a href="/guides/benefits-during-maternity-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">benefits during maternity leave</a>.</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Housing Benefit</strong> — MA may affect the amount you receive</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Council Tax Reduction</strong> — your local authority will take MA into account when calculating your entitlement</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong><a href="/guides/sure-start-maternity-grant/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Sure Start Maternity Grant</a></strong> — you can still apply for this one-off £500 payment if you are on qualifying benefits and expecting your first child</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you are on a low income, check whether you are eligible for Universal Credit alongside your Maternity Allowance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Can You Work While Receiving Maternity Allowance?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Yes! You are allowed up to <strong>10 Keeping in Touch (KIT) days</strong> without losing your Maternity Allowance. These days let you attend training sessions, come in for important meetings, or ease your transition back to work. Read our guide on <a href="/guides/keeping-in-touch-days-explained/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Keeping in Touch days explained</a> for more details on how KIT days work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you do any work beyond your 10 KIT days, you will lose your MA for each additional week in which you work.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Financial Planning on Maternity Allowance</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Because Maternity Allowance does not include a higher-rate period like SMP, your income drops to the flat rate from week one. It is important to start <a href="/guides/financial-planning-for-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">planning your finances</a> well before your maternity leave begins.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Consider building a savings buffer before your leave starts, and make sure you have claimed all the benefits you are entitled to, including <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a>. Use our <a href="/calculators/take-home-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Take-Home Pay Calculator</a> to understand your overall household income during maternity leave and read our guide on <a href="/guides/how-much-does-a-baby-cost-uk/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">how much does a baby cost in the UK</a> to budget for your new arrival.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Further Resources</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance Calculator</a> — check your exact entitlement</span></li>
