<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Leave When Your Baby Is Born Prematurely</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your baby arrives earlier than expected, your maternity rights still protect you — and in many ways, the law provides additional support. This guide covers everything you need to know about your leave, pay, and rights when your baby is born prematurely.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Automatic Maternity Leave Trigger</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your baby is born <strong>before your planned maternity leave start date</strong>, your maternity leave starts automatically on the <strong>day after the birth</strong>. This applies regardless of how early the baby arrives.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Key Rules:</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Maternity leave starts the day after the birth, not the day of birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>This applies even if you're still several weeks before your planned start date</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You must notify your employer as soon as is reasonably practicable</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your 52-week leave entitlement starts from this date</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">SMP Start Date</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your SMP also starts from the day after the birth. The full 39 weeks of SMP runs from this date, giving you the same total amount of pay regardless of when your baby arrives.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The Problem: NICU Time</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The biggest challenge for parents of premature babies has historically been that maternity leave "ticks away" while the baby is in hospital. A baby born at 28 weeks might spend 8-12 weeks in the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU). During this time, the mother is on maternity leave but her baby isn't home.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This means:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Weeks of paid maternity leave are used up before the baby comes home</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Parents have less time at home with their baby once discharged</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The financial and emotional pressure can be enormous</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Neonatal Care Leave: The New Solution (April 2025)</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">From <strong>6 April 2025</strong>, the new <a href="/guides/neonatal-care-leave-and-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Neonatal Care Leave and Pay</a> right gives parents up to <strong>12 additional weeks of leave</strong> when their baby needs neonatal care.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is <strong>on top of</strong> maternity leave, meaning:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>A mother of a premature baby can take up to <strong>64 weeks</strong> total leave (52 maternity + 12 neonatal)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Up to <strong>12 extra weeks of pay</strong> at £194.32/week (if eligible for statutory pay)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Both parents can take neonatal care leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Baby born at 30 weeks, spending 10 weeks in NICU:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Before April 2025:</strong> Maternity leave uses up 10 weeks while baby is in hospital → only 42 weeks left at home</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>After April 2025:</strong> Mother takes 52 weeks maternity leave + 10 weeks neonatal care leave = <strong>62 weeks total</strong>, with 52 weeks at home with baby</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">SMP Eligibility When Baby Arrives Early</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">A premature birth can affect SMP eligibility in specific ways:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">The Qualifying Week</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To qualify for SMP, you must have been employed for at least 26 weeks by the <strong>15th week before your due date</strong> (the qualifying week). If your baby arrives before the qualifying week:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You may <strong>still qualify</strong> if you've met the 26-week employment requirement by the actual week of birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you haven't met the requirement, your employer should issue an <strong>SMP1 form</strong> and you can apply for <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a> instead</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Average Weekly Earnings</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your AWE is calculated based on earnings in the 8-week period before the qualifying week. If your baby arrives before this calculation can be completed:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>HMRC uses the earnings information available up to the date of birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If you were on sick leave before the birth, this may affect your AWE</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Sick Leave Before a Premature Birth</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you were on <strong>pregnancy-related sick leave</strong> before your baby was born early:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If the sick leave started in the <strong>4 weeks before your due date</strong>, maternity leave would have been triggered automatically anyway</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>If the sick leave was <strong>more than 4 weeks</strong> before your due date, you would have been on normal sick leave/pay until the birth</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Either way, once the baby is born, maternity leave starts the next day.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Practical Steps When Your Baby Arrives Early</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Immediately</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Focus on your baby</strong> — your employer can wait</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Notify your employer</strong> as soon as is reasonably practicable (a partner or family member can do this for you)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span>Your employer should start SMP automatically</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Within the First Few Weeks</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Confirm your maternity leave dates</strong> with your employer in writing</span></li>
