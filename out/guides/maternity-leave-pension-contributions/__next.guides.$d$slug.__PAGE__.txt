<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What Happens to Your Pension During Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">One of the most overlooked aspects of maternity leave is the impact on your pension. The rules are actually more protective than many people realise — but there are important details you should know to avoid losing out.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Employer Pension Contributions During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">During <strong>paid maternity leave</strong> (the 39 weeks of SMP), your employer must continue making pension contributions based on your <strong>normal (pre-maternity) salary</strong> — not your reduced SMP amount.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is a crucial protection. Even though you might only be receiving £194.32 per week in SMP, your employer calculates their pension contribution as if you were still earning your full salary.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Example</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your salary is £35,000 and your employer contributes 5%:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Normal monthly contribution</strong>: £35,000 × 5% ÷ 12 = <strong>£145.83/month</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>During SMP</strong>: Employer still contributes <strong>£145.83/month</strong> (based on full salary)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer pays the same pension contribution throughout your paid maternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Your Own Pension Contributions During Maternity Leave</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your own contributions are different:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During <strong>paid maternity leave</strong>, your contributions are calculated based on your <strong>actual pay</strong> (SMP), not your full salary</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During <strong>unpaid maternity leave</strong> (weeks 40-52), neither you nor your employer need to make pension contributions (unless your scheme rules say otherwise)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This means your own contributions will be lower during SMP, but your employer's contributions remain at the full salary level.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Auto-Enrolment</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're auto-enrolled in a workplace pension, the same rules apply. Your employer must maintain their contributions at the full salary level during paid maternity leave.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">National Insurance Credits</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You automatically receive <strong>National Insurance credits</strong> during maternity leave, even if you're not paying NI contributions. These credits count towards your State Pension, ensuring your maternity leave doesn't create gaps in your National Insurance record.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">You get NI credits for:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The full 39 weeks of paid maternity leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Any weeks you receive Maternity Allowance</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>12 weeks of <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a> credits (once your baby is born)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Protecting Your State Pension</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">To receive the full new State Pension, you need <strong>35 qualifying years</strong> of National Insurance contributions. Maternity leave is protected in several ways:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>SMP counts</strong> — NI contributions are deducted from SMP above the threshold</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>NI credits</strong> — you receive credits during maternity leave and early years of parenting</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Child Benefit NI credits</strong> — if you claim Child Benefit and don't work, you receive NI credits until your youngest child is 12</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><strong>Important:</strong> Always claim Child Benefit even if you or your partner are subject to the <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">High Income Child Benefit Charge</a>. You can opt out of receiving the payment but still get the NI credit — which is worth approximately £275/year in State Pension.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Unpaid Maternity Leave (Weeks 40-52)</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">During unpaid maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your employer's pension contributions <strong>stop</strong> (unless your scheme is more generous)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Your pension contributions <strong>stop</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You still receive <strong>NI credits</strong> through Child Benefit</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This gap can be addressed by:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Making additional voluntary contributions (AVCs) when you return to work</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Checking if your employer offers any scheme to make up shortfalls</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Ensuring you claim Child Benefit for the NI credit</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Salary Sacrifice Pension Schemes</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you participate in a salary sacrifice pension arrangement, special rules apply during maternity leave:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During SMP, your employer must pay you at least the statutory minimum — they can't reduce your SMP through salary sacrifice</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Some employers will continue the sacrifice arrangement on a different basis during leave</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Check with your HR department about how your specific scheme handles maternity leave</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What About Enhanced Maternity Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your employer offers <a href="/calculators/enhanced-maternity-pay/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">enhanced maternity pay</a>, your pension contributions (both yours and your employer's) should be based on the actual enhanced pay during those weeks. This means your pension contributions may be higher than during SMP-only weeks.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Buying Back Missed Pension Contributions</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If your pension contributions were lower during maternity leave — or stopped entirely during unpaid leave — you may be able to make up the shortfall:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Workplace Pension Schemes</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Many pension providers allow you to make <strong>Additional Voluntary Contributions (AVCs)</strong> to top up your pension pot. This can be done as a lump sum or through increased monthly payments once you <a href="/guides/returning-to-work-after-maternity/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">return to work</a>. Some employers also offer <strong>matching schemes</strong>, where they'll match any extra contributions you make — effectively doubling your top-up.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Ask your pension scheme administrator about:
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Whether AVCs are available and any limits that apply</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Whether your employer offers matching contributions</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>The tax relief available on additional contributions (you get relief at your marginal rate)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Public Sector Pensions (NHS, Teachers, Civil Service)</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you're in a public sector defined benefit scheme such as the <a href="/guides/nhs-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">NHS pension</a> or <a href="/guides/teacher-maternity-pay-and-leave/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Teachers' Pension</a>, you may be able to <strong>buy back</strong> the pension you missed during unpaid maternity leave. Contact your pension scheme directly to find out the cost and deadline, as rules vary between schemes.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">State Pension Gaps</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you have gaps in your National Insurance record from periods without NI credits, you can make <strong>voluntary National Insurance contributions</strong> to fill them. Check your State Pension forecast at gov.uk to see whether you have any gaps. You usually have up to <strong>6 years</strong> to fill gaps through voluntary contributions.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Key Actions to Protect Your Pension</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">1.</span><span><strong>Check your pension scheme rules</strong> before maternity leave starts</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">2.</span><span><strong>Claim <a href="/calculators/child-benefit/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Child Benefit</a></strong> — even if you don't need the money, the NI credit is valuable</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">3.</span><span><strong>Consider AVCs</strong> after maternity leave to make up any shortfall</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">4.</span><span><strong>Request a pension forecast</strong> from your scheme administrator</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">5.</span><span><strong>Check your State Pension forecast</strong> at <a href="https://www.gov.uk/check-state-pension" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">gov.uk/check-state-pension</a></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-600 font-semibold">6.</span><span><strong>Ask about buying back</strong> missed contributions in defined benefit schemes</span></li>
