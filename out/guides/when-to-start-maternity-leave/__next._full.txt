<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">When Should You Start Maternity Leave?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Choosing when to start your maternity leave is one of the most personal decisions you'll make during pregnancy. There's no single "right" answer — it depends on your health, your job, your finances, and your preferences. This guide helps you weigh up the options.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">The Legal Limits</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The law sets clear boundaries on when maternity leave can begin:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Earliest start</strong>: 11 weeks before your due date (around week 29 of pregnancy)</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Latest start</strong>: The day after your baby is born</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Automatic triggers</strong>: Your leave starts automatically if your baby arrives before your chosen date, or if you're off sick with a pregnancy-related illness in the last 4 weeks before your due date</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Use our <a href="/calculators/maternity-leave-planner/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Leave Planner</a> to calculate your specific dates.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Starting Early (11-6 Weeks Before Due Date)</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Advantages</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>More time to rest and prepare before the birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Reduces work-related stress in late pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Time for nesting, hospital bag packing, and practical preparations</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Useful if your job is physically demanding or has a long commute</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Less pressure if your baby arrives early</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Disadvantages</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Less time off after your baby is born — your 52 weeks of leave starts from your chosen date, not the birth</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>SMP starts sooner, meaning your paid period ends sooner</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>You may feel bored or isolated if you're used to working</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Starting Late (2-0 Weeks Before Due Date)</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Advantages</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Maximum time off after the birth when you need it most</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>More weeks on full salary before dropping to SMP</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Keeps you engaged and distracted in late pregnancy</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>SMP payments extend further into your baby's first year</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Disadvantages</h3>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>Can be exhausting in the final weeks of pregnancy</span></li>
