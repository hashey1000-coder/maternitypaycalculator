<h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">How Much Maternity Pay Will I Get?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">This is the number one question every pregnant woman in the UK asks — and the answer depends on your employment status, earnings, and your employer's maternity policy. In this guide, we break down every possible scenario so you know exactly what to expect.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The UK provides two main statutory maternity payments: <strong>Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)</strong> paid by your employer, and <strong>Maternity Allowance (MA)</strong> paid by the government. Many employers also offer enhanced maternity pay on top of the statutory minimum.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Statutory Maternity Pay Rates for 2026/27</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you qualify for SMP, you'll receive:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 1-6</strong>: 90% of your average weekly earnings (AWE) — with no upper cap</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 7-39</strong>: £194.32 per week, or 90% of your AWE if that's lower</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Weeks 40-52</strong>: Unpaid (but you're still entitled to maternity leave)</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Your average weekly earnings are calculated using your gross pay over an 8-week reference period. This typically covers two monthly pay packets before the qualifying week (the 15th week before your due date).</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">SMP Examples at Different Salaries</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Here's what your total gross SMP looks like at common salary levels:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£20,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £346.15/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£8,254 total</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£25,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £432.69/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£8,773 total</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£30,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £519.23/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£9,528 total</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£35,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £605.77/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£10,047 total</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£40,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £692.31/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£10,331 total</strong></span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>£50,000/year</strong>: Weeks 1-6 at £865.38/week + Weeks 7-39 at £194.32/week = <strong>£11,605 total</strong></span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">For your exact figures, use our <a href="/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Pay Calculator</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">Maternity Allowance Rates</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">If you don't qualify for SMP — for example, if you're self-employed, recently changed jobs, or work through an agency — you may be entitled to <a href="/calculators/maternity-allowance/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">Maternity Allowance</a>.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">The MA rate for 2026/27 is:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Up to £194.32 per week</strong> for 39 weeks, or</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>90% of your average weekly earnings</strong> if that's less than £194.32</span></li></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">A key advantage of Maternity Allowance: it's <strong>completely tax-free</strong>. This means £194.32 per week goes directly into your bank with no deductions, unlike SMP which is subject to Income Tax and National Insurance.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">Read our <a href="/guides/maternity-allowance-guide/" class="text-violet-600 hover:underline font-medium">complete Maternity Allowance guide</a> for full eligibility details.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-3">What About Tax on Maternity Pay?</h2></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3">SMP is treated as earnings, so you'll pay Income Tax and National Insurance on it. However, because your income is lower during maternity leave, you may pay less tax than you expect:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During the <strong>flat-rate period</strong> (£194.32/week), your annualised income is about £9,733 — well below the Personal Allowance of £12,570</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span>During the <strong>90% period</strong>, you'll pay tax at your normal rate on those higher earnings</span></li>

Common packages include:</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Full pay for a set period</strong> — typically 12 to 26 weeks at full salary</span></li>
<li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>Topped-up SMP</strong> — employer pays the difference between SMP and your normal pay</span></li>

Check your employee handbook, contract, or HR department to find out what your employer offers.</p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><h3 class="text-lg font-semibold text-slate-800 mt-6 mb-2">Examples of Enhanced Maternity Pay</h3></p><p class="text-slate-600 leading-relaxed mb-3"><li class="flex items-start gap-2 ml-4"><span class="text-violet-400 mt-1">•</span><span><strong>NHS</strong>: 8 weeks full pay + 18 weeks half pay + 13 weeks SMP + 13 weeks unpaid</span></li>
